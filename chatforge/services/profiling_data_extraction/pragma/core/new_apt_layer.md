An interpretive layer that sits on top of PRAGMA measurements to explain *why* people engage in conversations, not just *how*.

This document is the current-state canonical spec. It consolidates four iterations of development:

## What Is APT

APT is an **interpretive layer** that sits on top of PRAGMA measurements. PRAGMA answers *how* — Involvement, control, density, intent, investment patterns. APT answers *why*.

**Architecture.** People can be profiled across three structural layers in the conversational domain:

- **Domain 1: Attachment** — why they stay
- **Self-Positioning** — a modulator between Domain 1 and Domain 2
- **Domain 2: Presentation** — how attachment is transmitted

The Self-Positioning modulator is what iteration-1 introduced as a named structural element; iterations 2–3.1 refined its internal structure. APT's original two-domain framing (Attachment + Presentation) is preserved; Self-Positioning modulates how Domain 1 signals land.

You are not adding Attachment as a dimension alongside PRAGMA's dimensions. You are building an inference system on top of PRAGMA outputs.

## The Three Structural Layers

### Domain 1: Attachment

Why they stay. Three variables:

1. **Charm** — how much they look up to you. Status, competence, high-value signals. Makes them want your approval.
2. **Hope** — belief they can get benefit from you. Key insight: high value alone isn't enough; they need to believe you'd actually share it. Accessible high value.
3. **Fear** — consequences of losing you. Power dynamics, secrets, dependency.

Without at least one of these, no connection forms.

Attachment applies to both sides. Asymmetry (who's chasing whom) is Domain 1 measured on both people, then compared.

### Self-Positioning — the Modulator

**Self-Positioning is a modulator between Domain 1 (Attachment) and Domain 2 (Presentation).** It operates multiplicatively on the three attachment variables — when it collapses, even high Charm/Hope/Fear signals get discounted post-hoc ("this guy doesn't deserve this"). This failure signature is called **devaluation**.

```
Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(non-extractive attention)
```

When `g` collapses, the product collapses regardless of how high `f` is. Each attachment variable is individually modulated:

- **Charm** under low Self-Positioning reads as shiny-but-hollow → charm is devalued
- **Hope** under low Self-Positioning reads as desperate-to-please → offered benefit loses perceived value
- **Fear** under low Self-Positioning reads as bluster → fails to consolidate into respect

#### Genus: Non-Extractive Attention

At the theoretical level, the axis that Self-Positioning tracks is **Non-Extractive Attention** — attention that is NOT oriented toward harvesting approval/validation/response from the current counterparty.

**The central organizing concept is outcome-independence** — attention whose resolution doesn't depend on the counterparty's response. This single property distinguishes productive non-extractive modes (all species below) from needy self-attention and Supplication.

#### Primary Species (first-class)

- **Self-Focus** = attention on own priorities whose resolution doesn't depend on the counterparty's response. The species most relevant to value-establishing contexts (professional outreach, dating first-contact, sales, networking).
- **Caregiver-Focus** = non-extractive attention on the counterparty's wellbeing, where the caring doesn't depend on their approval. Mothers, therapists, devoted teachers, nurses.

These two pass the orthogonality test cleanly — a person can be Self-Focused without being Caregiver-Focused and vice versa, with distinct observable correlates.

#### Secondary Species (refinements, applicable in specific contexts)

- **Mission-Focus** — attention on a larger cause / purpose (Gandhi, visionary founders, activists)
- **Task-Focus** — attention on the craft / work itself (elite performers, craftspeople in flow; from performance psychology)
- **Dialogue-Focus** — attention on a shared inquiry (Socratic dialogue, expert peer conversations)
- **Transcendent-Focus** — attention on an abstract ideal / god / truth (saints, contemplative practitioners)

#### Negative Pole

- **Supplication / Extractive Attention** = attention whose resolution IS dependent on the counterparty's response. Approval-harvesting. Collapses `g` in the gating formula. Produces devaluation.

#### Dual Character

Self-Positioning has two inseparable layers:

- **Internal** — the posture / bearing / orientation of attention. Where your attention genuinely lives. Not performable over long exposure.
- **Displayed** — the externally observable manifestation. The behaviors, signals, rhythm that the counterparty perceives. This is the **visibility principle** — internal state without observable manifestation doesn't produce the modulator effect.

Observer reads the displayed layer. The internal layer matters because performed displays without backing internal state are detected over time (see *The Narcissism Case* below).

#### Two-Level Naming

- **Theoretical level:** Non-Extractive Attention (genus) — used in theoretical discussions, APT Inference / APT Profiling outputs
- **User-facing level:** Self-Focus (primary species for value-establishing contexts) — used in LinkedIn diagnostic, practical application

Precedent from technical vocabulary: physics (force / push), medicine (myocardial infarction / heart attack), systems (latency / delay).

### Domain 2: Presentation

How attachment is transmitted. Three layers:

1. **Content** — what you say. Details, information, topics.
2. **Style** — how you say it. Word choice, length, structure, timing.
3. **Expressed frame** — the **investment-asymmetry** that your words imply. "I'm the selector" vs "please like me." Not internal — only matters as perceived.

**Note on Expressed Frame:** iteration-2 narrowed this from a bundled concept (investment-asymmetry + self-elevation) to investment-asymmetry only. The self-elevation component is now Self-Positioning (Self-Focus species at user-facing level). Expressed Frame and Self-Positioning are **orthogonal axes** — they combine into the 2×2 diagnostic below.

### What's Not a Domain But Matters

- **Internal frame (generic)** — not a domain as a blanket category. BUT: the Self-Positioning component of "internal frame" is load-bearing (iteration-3.1). The internal layer of Self-Positioning is causally necessary — performed external behaviors without the internal state misfire as try-hard, weird, or hostile over longer exposure.
- **Trajectory** — emerges when Domain 1 and Self-Positioning both work. Not a separate lever.
- **Timing/Rhythm** — part of Self-Positioning's display layer (Rhythm-Comfort signal) and part of style within Domain 2.

## Mechanism and Dynamics

### Multiplicative Gating (unchanged from iteration-1)

```
Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(non-extractive attention)
```

When `g` collapses, even high `f` produces near-zero real attachment. Failure signature: **devaluation**.

### Temporal Precedence (iteration-3.1)

The formula operates at every moment, but `g` and `f` have different temporal profiles:

- **`g` operates at instantaneous display-bandwidth** — posture, voice tone, opening premise, eye contact, response calibration, exit-willingness are all readable within seconds
- **`f` develops over longer time-horizons** — Charm/Hope/Fear need content to accumulate before full signal

**Implication:** in short windows (first 5-30 seconds of first-contact), `g` dominates the attachment-probability because `f` hasn't fully developed. This is why 5-minute approaches are decided by Self-Positioning display rather than by Charm depth. Same formula, different temporal signal-density ratio.

### Oscillation Dynamics (iteration-3.1)

Healthy interactions oscillate between species within exchanges. In established relationships, sustained pure Self-Focus reads as cold / indifferent; sustained pure Caregiver-Focus reads as dependent / sycophantic. Healthy rhythm alternates. Sustained imbalance in either direction signals pathology.

Formal modeling (rhythm parameters, optimal frequencies) is deferred to future work.

### Species × Attachment-Variable Amplification

Different species amplify different attachment variables particularly:
- Self-Focus amplifies all three evenly
- Mission-Focus amplifies Hope (followers hope to be part of the mission)
- Caregiver-Focus amplifies Charm via admiration (others admire the caregiving)

(Empirical validation pending — flagged in Open Questions.)

## The Displayed Self-Positioning Layer

**Displayed Self-Positioning** is the visibility principle operationalized — internal Non-Extractive Attention must manifest observably to produce the modulator effect. The 5-signal catalog (with 12 operational metrics across text-mode and multimodal channels) is what PRAGMA detects.

### The 5-Signal Catalog

| Signal | Definition | High-g signal | Low-g signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Withholding-Signal** | Negative space — what the speaker chose not to say | Present (comfortable not-elaborating when invited) | Absent (over-elaborates everywhere) |
| **Premise-Posture** | Opening frame | Selecting / offering / asserting | Requesting-permission |
| **Self-Justification-Density** | Clauses-per-message explaining why speaking | Low | High |
| **Exit-Willingness** | Risk taken in the move | High (disagreement, selectivity, terminable-close) | Low (safety-optimized, hedged, open-ended) |
| **Rhythm-Comfort** | Comfort with silence / delay | Present (unrushed) | Rushed, filler-laden |

### 6 Text-Feature Operational Metrics

| Metric | Computes | Links to signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening verb class** | Self-action vs other-request | Premise-Posture |
| **Pronoun ratio** (I/we vs you/your) | Balanced or you-heavy | Premise-Posture, Self-Justification-Density |
| **Question-to-statement ratio** | Structural openness | Self-Justification-Density |
| **Self-justification clause count** | Direct measurement | Self-Justification-Density |
| **Elaboration gradient** | Word count per point | Withholding-Signal |
| **Closing assumption tone** | "Take it or leave it" vs "waiting for response" | Exit-Willingness |

### 6 Multimodal Operational Metrics (iteration-3.1)

For face-to-face and embodied contexts:

| Metric | Measures | High-g signal | Low-g signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Posture** | Body-language grounding | Relaxed, upright, own-centered | Fidgety, closed, leaning-toward |
| **Voice cadence / pitch** | Speech-pattern self-regulation | Calm, unrushed, lower-pitched | Rushed, pitched-up, apologetic |
| **Eye contact pattern** | Gaze-regulation | Steady, unhurried, direct | Darting, avoiding, over-eager |
| **Body alignment** | Spatial orientation | Oriented at from confident stance | Looming-toward, pursuing-with-body |
| **Response-timing tempo** | Reactivity tempo | Unhurried response; comfortable pauses | Immediate-reactive; no silence tolerance |
| **Exit readiness** | Physical comfort with ending | Comfortable with natural close | Clingy, extending, reluctant |

Together: **12 operational metrics** across text-mode and embodied channels. Deeper multimodal-specification (detection rules, cross-modal weighting, edge cases) flagged for iteration-3.3.

## The Narcissism Case

Narcissism does NOT contradict the theory. At the moment-to-moment interaction level — where the modulator operates — narcissistic attention IS self-focused (attention on own greatness, story, image). Narcissism fits the Self-Focus species mechanically.

**What makes narcissist-follower devotion exaggerated** is additive mechanisms OUTSIDE APT's scope:

- Intermittent reinforcement (variable-ratio reward schedules)
- Grandiosity signaling (high-status display)
- Social-norm violation (signals independence from ordinary constraints)
- Love-bombing (investment-asymmetry created early)
- Trauma bonding (abuse-reward neurochemistry)

**Temporal arc — why narcissist-follower relationships burn out:** Non-Extractive Attention is a **costly signal** — genuine non-extractive behavior requires foregoing optimization for the current exchange. Observers read the cost and infer reliability. Narcissists run a **mimicry strategy** — they display Non-Extractive Attention without paying the full internal cost. Short term: mimicry produces similar attachment to genuine non-extraction. Long term: the cost-not-actually-paid is detected; inconsistencies surface; attachment decays; followers eventually leave.

**Pro-social framing (prescriptive, not descriptive):** cultivate **genuine** non-extractive attention, not mimicked displays. At the mechanism level, the theory is indifferent to healthy vs pathological — both produce similar immediate attachment. At the sustainability level, they diverge — genuine produces sustained attachment; mimicry produces attachment-decay.

## Adjacent Mechanisms (Outside APT Scope)

APT describes the Self-Positioning modulator mechanism. Several real attachment-generating mechanisms are **adjacent** but outside APT. They co-occur with Self-Positioning (particularly in extreme cases) but operate by different mechanisms:

1. **Intermittent reinforcement** — variable-ratio reward schedules
2. **Grandiosity signaling** — high-status / competence display
3. **Social-norm violation** — "I'm not bound by what binds ordinary people"
4. **Scarcity of access** — limited availability raises perceived value
5. **Progressive commitment** — small yes → large yes (foot-in-door)
6. **Trauma bonding** — abuse-reward cycles via neurochemistry
7. **Social proof** — existing devotees evidence target's value
8. **Isolation** — removing alternative attachment figures
9. **Aspirational distance** — gap between self and target creates desire

**Scoping (ecological framing):** these are *signal-exploitation strategies evolutionarily adjacent to genuine non-extractive attention — parasitic or predatory at the attention-economy level*. Future APT-extension inquiries may formalize some as additional modulators if warranted by evidence.

## User-Facing Diagnostics

### The 2×2 Typology

Self-Positioning × Investment-Asymmetry (iteration-1 structure, preserved):

| | Low Investment (stepping back) | High Investment (leaning in) |
|---|---|---|
| **High Self-Focus** | Confident Selector | **Respected Expert** ← target |
| **Low Self-Focus** | Disengaged / Avoidant | **Try-Hard / Supplicating** ← primary failure mode |

The vertical axis label is Self-Focus at user-facing level; Non-Extractive Attention at theoretical level.

### The 5-Question Audit (iteration-2, for LinkedIn / value-establishing contexts)

Quick diagnostic for any outgoing message:

1. Does my premise request permission or select/offer?
2. How many clauses explain why I'm writing?
3. Do I elaborate beyond what the point requires?
4. Does my close allow them to not reply?
5. Am I willing to not get a response? (And does that show?)

### The Tiered Calibration Audit (iteration-3.1, for multi-context finer calibration)

**Gateway 1-question audit:**

> **"Am I okay if they don't respond favorably?"**
>
> Genuine YES → non-extractive attention likely well-calibrated; continue.
> NO or unsure → use the full 7-point audit.
>
> *Important:* gateway-YES is provisional. If outcomes surprise, run the full audit — self-deception in 1-question form is a real risk.

**Full 7-point audit (independent-composite):**

1. **Context check** — What context am I in? (value-establishing / established / hierarchical / caring / performance / cause-driven / dialogue / contemplative)
2. **Species check** — Which non-extractive-attention species is active for me?
3. **Species-context fit** — Is this species appropriate for this context? (See species-context-fit table below.)
4. **Outcome-independence** — Am I okay if they don't respond favorably? *(the decisive test)*
5. **Internal state** — Was I grounded or anxious during the exchange?
6. **Counterparty-response curve** — Was their attention rising or falling through the exchange?
7. **Exchange quality** — Mutual benefit or extraction / drain?

If ALL pass → high confidence. If ANY fails → that check identifies the specific miscalibration dimension.

## Prescriptive Guidance Layer

### Species-Context-Fit

Prescriptive guidance about which species to cultivate for intended outcomes:

| Context | Primary species | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Value-establishing (approach, cold outreach, sales first-contact) | **Self-Focus** | LinkedIn use case |
| Established trusting relationships | **Caregiver-Focus** with Self-Focus oscillation | Healthy rhythm |
| Hierarchical / professional | **Task-Focus** or **Self-Focus** | Context-role dependency |
| Caring / parenting / mentoring | **Caregiver-Focus** | Non-extractive attention on growth |
| Performance / craft | **Task-Focus** | Performance-psychology pedigree |
| Cause-driven leadership | **Mission-Focus** | Visionary-leader archetype |
| Philosophical / intellectual dialogue | **Dialogue-Focus** | Shared-inquiry mode |
| Contemplative / spiritual | **Transcendent-Focus** | Rare in professional contexts |

Prescriptive (not descriptive) — guidance about choice, not empirical claim about emergence.

### Practice Protocol References

One-line pointers to mature traditions for cultivating non-extractive attention:

- **Stoic dichotomy of control** (Epictetus; Marcus Aurelius) — daily journaling and evening-review directly cultivate outcome-independence
- **Performance-psychology task-focus** — pre-performance routines, process cues, body-scan grounding as Self-Focus cultivation tools
- **Rogers' Unconditional Positive Regard** — congruence + UPR + empathic understanding operationalize Caregiver-Focus
- **Buddhist non-grasping attention / Zen beginner's mind** — parallel concepts (one-line mention)

These are references, not framework imports. APT stays descriptive; practice traditions are pointed to externally.

### Context-Appropriate Caveats

Low Self-Focus is a failure mode in value-establishing contexts. It is **appropriate and healthy** in:

- **Genuine vulnerability / authentic need** — asking for help you actually need, expressed transparently
- **Transparent power asymmetry** — intern-to-CEO, student-to-professor in domain
- **Intimate / high-warmth conversations** — deep conversations where mutual other-focus is the target
- **Ritual / ceremonial contexts** — condolences, apologies, formal gratitude

The modulator's relevance is context-scoped.

## Character-Perception Scoping

Across repeated interactions, an individual's stable tendencies in non-extractive-attention expression — their species bearing + the level they operate at — become part of what observers recognize as the person's character. APT's trait-with-bearings structure captures this **Self-Positioning dimension** of character perception.

**Character in full is broader than APT's scope.** Character includes values, moral disposition, cognitive style, specific choices. Self-Positioning is particularly visible because every interaction exhibits it, which disproportionately weights it in character-perception — but APT doesn't claim to cover character broadly. APT Profiling captures the Self-Positioning component; the rest is outside APT.

## Two Orders: Causal vs Analytical

**Causal order** (what's actually happening):

```
Self-Positioning  →  Domain 1 (Attachment) / Domain 2 (Presentation)
```

Self-Positioning is upstream of both. It causes both how the counterparty's attachment responds (Domain 1 in them) and how your presentation is executed (Domain 2 by you). The iteration-1 Domain 1 → Domain 2 causal flow holds *downstream* of Self-Positioning.

**Analytical order** (what you can measure):

```
PRAGMA measures presentation (observable)
       ↓
Self-Positioning display inferred from signal catalog
       ↓
Patterns accumulate over time
       ↓
Attachment inferred (theory layer)
```

Early in a conversation, you only have Domain 2 data plus Displayed Self-Positioning (which is visible in seconds — see Temporal Precedence). As more data accumulates, the attachment inference becomes more confident.

Early attachment reads can be wrong. Someone might present as charmed (high interest) but actually be qualifying you. The pattern over time reveals the truth. Self-Positioning display tends to stabilize faster than attachment inference, which helps early estimates.

## How PRAGMA Maps to APT

### PRAGMA → Domain 2 (Presentation)

Almost direct translation:

- **Content** ← Dialogic Function, Information Density, Conversational Intent
- **Style** ← Expressed Involvement, Temporal Structure, Investment
- **Expressed frame (investment-asymmetry)** ← Control Distribution, Investment asymmetry

### PRAGMA → Self-Positioning (Displayed)

The 5-signal catalog + 12 operational metrics:

- **Withholding-Signal** ← low-density responses when context invites elaboration; Elaboration Gradient metric
- **Premise-Posture** ← Opening Verb Class, Pronoun Ratio metrics; Dialogic Function opening intent
- **Self-Justification-Density** ← Pronoun Ratio (I/we vs you/your), Self-justification clause count; Dialogic Function explaining
- **Exit-Willingness** ← Closing Assumption Tone; Investment patterns; Control Distribution willingness to assert
- **Rhythm-Comfort** ← Temporal Structure signals (pauses, response-timing tempo)

Multimodal metrics (6 more) require multimodal extensions of PRAGMA (future — iteration-3.3).

### PRAGMA → Domain 1 (Attachment)

Inference over time. Not directly visible, but deducible:

- **Charm** ← sustained Expressed Involvement, Control Distribution tilted toward you, they seek your approval (Dialogic Function: Querying, Affirming toward you)
- **Hope** ← repeated engagement on specific topics, high Expressed Involvement when discussing what you can offer, forward-looking Intent
- **Fear** ← asymmetric Control they don't challenge, careful word choices, reluctance to disengage even when disinterested

### The Hierarchy

```
PRAGMA (raw measurement, 7 dimensions)
       ↓
Dynamics Profile (composed description of segment dynamics)
       ↓
Interpretation Layer (tensions, hidden dynamics)
       ↓
       ├→ Domain 2 (presentation patterns — what they're doing)
       ├→ Displayed Self-Positioning (5-signal catalog + 12 metrics)
       └→ Domain 1 (attachment inference — why they're here)
              ↑
              modulated by Self-Positioning
```

## Three Outputs from PRAGMA

PRAGMA produces three parallel outputs:

| Output | Focus | What It Reveals |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| **Behavioral Profiling** | Individual | Communication style patterns (PRAGMA direct) |
| **APT Inference** | Relational | Dynamics between specific participants, including Non-Extractive Attention species + level |
| **APT Profiling** | Individual | Attachment bearings + Self-Positioning bearings + presentation tendencies (aggregated from APT Inference observations) |

### Behavioral Profiling

Individual communication patterns derived directly from PRAGMA, without APT interpretation.

- Fact-based profiling: "This person is a developer in Berlin who likes AI."
- Behavioral profiling: "This person consistently operates from high investment, asks qualifying questions, maintains symmetric control, escalates involvement slowly."

Relational and dynamic, not static.

### APT Inference

Relational dynamics between a specific participant pair. What's happening between Person A and Person B right now.

**Output structure:**
- Directional Charm/Hope/Fear (5-level categorical, per-direction)
- Directional Non-Extractive Attention: **species** (Self-Focus / Caregiver-Focus / Mission-Focus / Task-Focus / Dialogue-Focus / Transcendent-Focus / Supplication) + **level** (high / calibrated / low / collapsed)
- Outcome-independence estimate (high / medium / low)
- Displayed Self-Positioning signature (which of the 5 signals + 12 metrics are active/absent)
- Presentation descriptions (Content / Style / Expressed Frame)
- Per-field reasons

### APT Profiling

Individual attachment bearings + Self-Positioning bearings, aggregated from multiple APT Inference observations across relationships.

**Output structure:**
- **Attachment Bearings** — what triggers their attachment (what charms them, what they hope for, what they fear)
- **Self-Positioning Bearings** — which species they tend toward; their central outcome-independence level; context-dependent species tendencies
- **Presentation Tendencies** — how they present under different conditions

Example profile:
- "Attachment: gets charmed by expertise, hopes for mentorship, fears rejection"
- "Self-Positioning: Self-Focus-dominant in professional contexts, Caregiver-Focus-dominant with close friends; baseline outcome-independence MEDIUM; collapses to Supplication under high-status counterparties"
- "Presentation: frame collapses when challenged, over-explains when insecure, goes terse when confident"

Behavioral Profiling tells you how someone talks. APT Profiling tells you what moves them — their vulnerabilities, motivations, levers, and the stable posture from which they engage.

## Why This Architecture Is Smart

- PRAGMA stays clean and empirical
- Self-Positioning layer is modular — iteration-3.1 refinements didn't require restructuring PRAGMA or Domain 1
- Attachment interpretation can be wrong — but it's a separate layer, so errors don't contaminate base measurements
- Multiple interpretive frameworks could sit on top of PRAGMA (APT is one; others could exist)
- The genus/species taxonomy extends — new species can be added without restructure
- Adjacent mechanisms are scoped out explicitly — APT doesn't over-claim; extensions can absorb them if warranted

- Multiple additional modulators surface and all turn out to be attention-based
- Adjacent mechanisms reveal themselves to be attention-pattern variants rather than independent phenomena
