## Cycle 2 — Probe Coherence-family

### What it is

Consistency of the person across dimensions:
- **Temporal Coherence** — same person across time (not wildly different day-to-day)
- **Cross-Context Coherence** — same person across contexts (home/work/social)
- **Narrative Coherence** — life-story hangs together internally
- **Values Coherence** — stated vs observed values align
- **Authenticity** — surface expression matches internal state

### Sub-structure analysis

These consolidate into **two main aspects:**

1. **Stability Coherence** = temporal + cross-context consistency
2. **Alignment Coherence** = surface-depth match (authenticity + values-behavior alignment)

These are distinct: a stable hypocrite (consistently hypocritical) has stability but not alignment. A fluctuating sincere person has alignment in the moment but not stability.

### Qualification test

| Criterion | Verdict | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Multiplicative on attachment? | **YES** | Observer can't form stable mental model of erratic person → can't attach; hypocrisy-detected collapses trust regardless of other signals |
| 2. Dual character? | **YES** | Internal (who you actually are consistently) + displayed (observable across interactions) |
| 3. Orthogonal to Self-Positioning? | **YES** | Narcissists are often Self-Focused but incoherent (explains follower burnout partially); consistent caring mother is non-Self-Focused but highly Coherent |
| 4. Not reducible to Self-Positioning species? | **YES** | Coherence is about consistency of expression, not what species you express |
| 5. Not reducible to adjacent mechanism? | **YES** | Operates at modulator-level, not external pattern |
| 6. Observable signals? | **YES** | Longitudinal interaction reveals consistency; linguistic tells; cross-context reports |
| 7. Failure signature? | **YES** | "Erratic" / "can't pin down" / "doesn't seem real" → observer cannot form stable mental model → **Model-Collapse** failure |
| 8. Evidence-based? | **YES** | Trust-formation literature; relationship research on consistency; narrative identity (Ricoeur, Bruner) |

**Strong candidate for modulator status.** 7 of 8 criteria clearly pass; 1 (failure signature) identified with specific mechanism.

### Attention-based analysis (Cluster 4 test)

Is Coherence attention-based?

- Temporal Coherence → partially attention-related (does your attention stay on the same priorities over time?) but also identity-related (do you have a stable self?)
- Cross-Context Coherence → partially attention (attention direction across contexts) but also personality/role-related
- Alignment Coherence → NOT primarily attention; about integrity of inner experience matching outer expression

**Verdict: partially attention-related but NOT purely attention-based.** Coherence has identity/personality/integrity components independent of attention direction.

### Signals generated

- **Strong modulator candidate:** Coherence
- **Model-Collapse** as named failure signature (like devaluation for Self-Positioning)
- **NOT purely attention-based** → relevant for Cluster 4 prediction

## Cycle 3 — Probe Emotional Composure

### What it is

Capacity to regulate emotional state under pressure — to stay present and functional when stressed, challenged, or disrupted. Distinct from Affect (emotional valence you bring); this is about regulation, not content.

### Qualification test

| Criterion | Verdict | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Multiplicative? | **YES** | Dysregulated person under stress loses attachment regardless of other signals; emotional chaos collapses even high Charm |
| 2. Dual character? | **YES** | Internal nervous-system state + displayed composure |
| 3. Orthogonal to Self-Positioning? | **YES** | Anxious-self-focused person (inward-directed but dysregulated); calm-other-focused person (composed while attending to other) |
| 4. Not reducible to species? | **YES** | Regulation is about emotional state management, not attention direction |
| 5. Not reducible to adjacent? | **YES** | Operates at modulator level |
| 6. Observable? | **YES** | Voice pitch under stress, breathing pattern, body language, recovery from disruption, response to conflict |
| 7. Failure signature? | **YES** | "Unstable" / "reactive" / "draining" → observer feels emotional load transferred to them → **Contagion-Drain** failure |
| 8. Evidence? | **YES** | Emotional-regulation research (Gross), attachment theory (secure vs anxious), what makes people "safe" to be around |

**Strong candidate for modulator status.** 8 of 8 criteria pass.

### Attention-based analysis

Is Emotional Composure attention-based?

- One aspect: where attention goes under stress (on stressor vs meta-regulating, ruminating vs observing)
- Another aspect: physiological regulation (nervous-system state, breath, body) — NOT attention
- Another aspect: emotional processing (how emotions are metabolized) — NOT purely attention

**Verdict: partially attention-related, not purely attention-based.** Significant non-attention components (physiological, neurochemical, developmental).

### Signals generated

- **Strong modulator candidate:** Emotional Composure
- **Contagion-Drain** as failure signature
- **NOT purely attention-based**

## Cycle 4 — Probe Resonance

### What it is

Shared world-model / genuine mutual recognition ("you get me"). Emerges from value/worldview compatibility, intellectual/emotional match, shared reference points.

### Qualification test — CRITICAL AMBIGUITY

| Criterion | Verdict | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Multiplicative on attachment? | **AMBIGUOUS** | Does Resonance AMPLIFY Charm/Hope/Fear, or does it generate attachment INDEPENDENTLY of them? |
| 2. Dual character? | YES | Internal (actual worldview) + displayed (spontaneous match in interaction) |
| 3. Orthogonal to Self-Positioning? | YES | Can have Self-Focus with or without Resonance |
| 4. Reducible to Dialogue-Focus species? | PARTIAL | Dialogue-Focus is SPECIES-level; Resonance might be the observer-side complement |
| 5. Observable? | YES | Agreement rate, language convergence, shared-reference comfort |
| 6. Failure signature? | YES | "Talking past each other" / "not on same page" → attachment cools into formality |
| 7. Evidence? | YES | Friendship / intellectual partnership / long-marriage research |

### The critical question

**Is Resonance a MODULATOR or a SEPARATE ATTACHMENT DIMENSION?**

Consider: two people can have zero Charm/Hope/Fear between them but still attach deeply through Resonance (think of finding someone who really gets your niche interest — no status games, no hope-for-benefit, just recognition). If Resonance can generate attachment WITHOUT any of the three, then it's NOT modulating them — it's a separate axis.

Alternative: Resonance could be a **4th attachment variable** in Domain 1 alongside Charm/Hope/Fear.

This is structurally important. If Domain 1 needs expansion from 3 to 4 variables, that's a different architectural change than adding a modulator.

### Signals generated

- **CRITICAL FINDING:** Resonance might NOT be a modulator — it might be a 4th attachment variable requiring Domain 1 expansion
- **Ambiguity forwarded to sensemaking** for classification
- If 4th attachment variable: affects Domain 1 structure, not modulator landscape

- Moment-level rhythm (captured by Rhythm-Comfort)

**Verdict: PARTIALLY REDUCIBLE to Self-Positioning's Rhythm-Comfort signal.** The remaining aspects (interaction-level cadence, relationship-level tempo) are too thin to justify a separate modulator.

Same analysis: CONTEXT property, not person property. Affects how attachment forms but not a person-level modulator.

**Verdict: NOT A MODULATOR.** Timing Momentum is a situational factor.
