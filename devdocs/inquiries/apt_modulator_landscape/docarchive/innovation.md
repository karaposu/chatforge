### 1.B Focused — Signal-catalog temporal differences

**Implication:** the signal-catalog format should NOT be uniform across the Suite. Spec should acknowledge:
- SP and EC have instantaneous signal catalogs (single-interaction detection)
- Coherence has longitudinal signal detection (multi-interaction aggregation)

This is a structural refinement for PRAGMA integration — Coherence requires cross-segment aggregation, not per-message inference.

## Mechanism 2 — Combination

### 2.A Generic — Signal overlap across modulators

At PRAGMA level, some raw signals inform multiple modulators:

- **Voice stability over a segment** → informs EC (regulation) AND Coherence (consistency)
- **Pronoun ratio** → informs SP Self-Justification-Density AND potentially Coherence (consistent self-reference)
- **Elaboration gradient** → informs SP Withholding-Signal AND potentially Coherence (consistent engagement level)
- **Response latency patterns** → informs SP Rhythm-Comfort AND EC Recovery aspect

**Implication:** PRAGMA signals are SHARED across modulator inference. The inference layer maps PRAGMA → each modulator via weighted combinations, with the same raw signal feeding multiple modulators.

This doesn't violate orthogonality — modulators are conceptually orthogonal (independent dimensions) but their OPERATIONAL detection may share input signals. Analogous to how height and weight are conceptually orthogonal but both inferable from a photo.

### 2.B Focused — Resonance × Dialogue-Focus interaction

Structural clarification needed: Resonance (dyadic emergent) vs Dialogue-Focus (individual species).

**When both parties are in Dialogue-Focus on the same object** → Resonance emerges between them. Mutual non-extractive attention on shared inquiry generates recognition.

**When one party is in Dialogue-Focus, other is in Self-Focus (engaged listener)** → No Resonance in this interaction. But Dialogue-Focus still operates as non-extractive attention for the first party; Self-Focus operates as non-extractive attention for the second.

**When both parties share worldview but aren't in Dialogue-Focus species** (e.g., both in Self-Focus but on compatible agendas) → Resonance can still emerge from the compatibility, not from shared Dialogue-Focus specifically.

**Clean structural relationship:**
- Dialogue-Focus = one party's individual species (how their attention operates)
- Resonance = dyadic attachment variable (emergent when values/worldviews/attention-objects align)

These interact but aren't reducible to each other. Spec should state this explicitly.

### 2.C Contrarian — All 3 modulators as one "Reliability Signal"

What if Self-Positioning + Coherence + Emotional Composure all track ONE underlying property — "reliability"?

- SP = reliable non-extractive attention (doesn't harvest approval)
- Coherence = reliable consistency (doesn't shapeshift)
- EC = reliable emotional regulation (doesn't spiral)

All three signal to observer: "this person is predictable in a specific way" → attachment forms because predictability enables modeling.

**Unification attempt:** collapse 3 modulators into 1 "Reliability" modulator with 3 sub-dimensions (attention-reliability, consistency-reliability, emotional-reliability).

**Counter:** each has a DISTINCT failure signature:
- SP failure: devaluation (attention seems extractive)
- Coherence failure: Model-Collapse (can't form stable mental model)
- EC failure: Contagion-Drain (emotional load transferred)

One-modulator theory would lose diagnostic differentiation. Observers experience these collapses DIFFERENTLY; unifying them loses the practical distinction.

**Verdict: KEEP 3 distinct modulators.** But acknowledge in the Modulator Suite introduction that they share "reliability" as common theme. This gives the Suite conceptual unity while preserving diagnostic differentiation.

## Mechanism 3 — Inversion

### 3.A Generic — Invert "new modulators are additive"

What if adding Coherence + EC REPLACES something in iteration-3?

Testing specific replacements:
- **Does Coherence subsume signaling-theory mimicry?** No — they're different detection mechanisms. Mimicry = cost-not-paid detection. Coherence = inconsistency-across-contexts detection. Both can apply to narcissism (the same phenomenon detected by two lenses).
- **Do Coherence or EC replace any of the 9 adjacent mechanisms?** Quick check: intermittent reinforcement, grandiosity, etc. are interaction patterns / environmental factors, not person-level modulators. Not replaceable.

**Implication: nothing replaced.** iteration-3.2 is purely additive. This preserves iteration-3's architectural commitments unchanged. Good for continuity.

### 3.B Focused — Invert "modulators are orthogonal"

Sensemaking claimed orthogonality via dissociation tests (consistent hothead = coherent + dysregulated; calm liar = composed + incoherent). But claim is theoretical; what about empirical?

In practice, the 3 modulators might be highly correlated:
- Consistent people (Coherence) tend to be emotionally regulated (EC) — common causes: maturity, self-knowledge, stability
- Self-focused people (SP) tend to be consistent (Coherence) — because outcome-independence requires stable identity

**Empirical correlation is likely.** But this is a separate question from theoretical orthogonality. Analogous: height and weight are theoretically independent but empirically correlated in humans.

**Implication:** spec should state THEORETICAL orthogonality (dissociation possible at definition level) without claiming EMPIRICAL INDEPENDENCE (real-world correlation expected). Precise epistemic language matters.

### 3.C Contrarian (system-level) — Invert "iteration labels are theoretical commitments"

What if "iteration-3.2" vs "iteration-4" labeling is just a communication convention, not a theoretical commitment?

**System-level view:** iteration numbering serves the inquiry trail for cross-session continuity. The IMPORTANT questions are:
- Does this update preserve or break prior commitments?
- Is the architectural change coherent?
- Does evidence support the expansion?

Labels are downstream of these.

**Implication:** don't over-think labeling. If iteration-3.2 is the right label by convention (architectural expansion, not substrate reframe), use it. But don't treat the label itself as a structural claim.

#### Coherence specification (P1)

**Definition:** "Coherence is the consistency of a person's non-extractive-attention expression across time and contexts (Stability aspect) combined with the alignment between surface expression and internal state (Alignment aspect). Observers infer Coherence from longitudinal observation; when it fails, observers cannot form a stable mental model of the person → attachment cannot consolidate regardless of other signals."

**Aspects:**
- **Stability Coherence** — same person across time and across different contexts (work, home, social, crisis)
- **Alignment Coherence** — surface expression matches internal state (what you say/do matches what you believe/feel)

**Failure signature: Model-Collapse** — observer's attempt to form a stable predictive model of the person fails due to inconsistencies or misalignments; attachment discounts as a result.

**Orthogonality:**
- vs Self-Positioning: narcissists are often Self-Focused at moment-to-moment but incoherent across contexts (this is the Coherence-based refinement of iteration-3's narcissism reconciliation)
- vs Emotional Composure: consistent hothead = Coherent + dysregulated; calm liar = composed + incoherent

**Signal Catalog (3-tier longitudinal structure):**

*Inference signals (longitudinal, 3-5):*
- **Cross-Context Stability** — same core behavior/priorities across work/home/social
- **Value-Action Alignment** — stated values match observed actions
- **Temporal Self-Consistency** — same person over weeks/months (not wild fluctuations)
- **Role-Boundary Consistency** — roles taken on and dropped consistently
- **Commitment-Follow-Through** — stated intentions followed through

*Text-feature metrics (longitudinal, cross-message aggregation):*
- Value-vocabulary consistency across messages
- Topic-continuity across conversations
- Tone consistency (not shifting dramatically per audience)

*Multimodal signatures (longitudinal):*
- Posture/voice consistency across video calls over time
- Dress/grooming consistency (subject to context-appropriateness)

**PRAGMA → Coherence mapping hypothesis:** requires cross-segment aggregation rather than per-message inference. Dialogic Function + Information Density aggregated over time + cross-context comparison.

**Cultivation references:** Stoic journaling; ACT values clarification; McAdams narrative identity research; Aristotelian virtue ethics; Ricoeur narrative self; integrity disciplines.

**Relationship to iteration-3's narcissism reconciliation:** Coherence-failure-via-Model-Collapse provides an upstream mechanism for the signaling-theory mimicry arc (mimicry's temporal decay IS detected via Coherence-failure). Complementary, not replacement.

#### Emotional Composure specification (P2)

**Definition:** "Emotional Composure is the capacity to regulate emotional and physiological state under pressure — staying present and functional when stressed, challenged, or disrupted. Includes physiological regulation (body, voice, breath), affective regulation (emotional valence), and recovery speed (bouncing back from disruption)."

**Aspects:**
- **Physiological Regulation** — body, voice, breath under stress
- **Affective Regulation** — emotional valence stability
- **Recovery Speed** — time to return to baseline after disruption

**Failure signature: Contagion-Drain** — dysregulation transfers emotional load to observer; observer feels drained/burdened/uncomfortable; attachment signals discount or collapse.

**Orthogonality:**
- vs Self-Positioning: anxious-Self-Focused (inward but dysregulated); calm-Caregiver-Focused (composed and attending)
- vs Coherence: consistent hothead (Coherent + dysregulated); calm liar (composed + Incoherent)

**Signal Catalog (3-tier instantaneous structure — parallel to SP):**

- **Recovery Latency** — time from disruption to baseline

- **De-escalation Capacity** — ability to stay composed when other party escalates

*Text-feature metrics (stress-responsive):*
- Word choice under pressure (calm vs reactive)
- Response-latency patterns when challenged
- Punctuation/capitalization under stress
- Escalation vs de-escalation language
- Recovery markers in message threads

*Multimodal metrics (instantaneous):*

- Body tension patterns

**PRAGMA → Emotional Composure mapping:** Temporal Structure signals (response-latency variance under stress); affect-laden Dialogic Function patterns; voice/video analysis for multimodal.

#### Resonance integration (P3)

**Definition:** "Resonance is genuine mutual recognition between two parties — sharing world-models, values, frameworks, or ways of seeing. It emerges dyadically when both parties' internal maps align enough for 'you get me' to register; it is NOT a property of either individual alone."

**Domain 1 role:** 4th attachment variable (joining Charm, Hope, Fear). Generates attachment additively within the f function.

**Updated formula:** `Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear, resonance) × g₁(SP) × g₂(Coherence) × g₃(Emotional Composure)`

**Test case:** two strangers discover a deeply unusual shared interest (e.g., medieval Ottoman financial instruments). Instant connection. No status games (both non-experts), no benefit-seeking (non-commercial), no fear (strangers). Attachment forms via mutual recognition alone. Charm/Hope/Fear = zero; Resonance generates attachment.

**Structural distinction from Dialogue-Focus:**
