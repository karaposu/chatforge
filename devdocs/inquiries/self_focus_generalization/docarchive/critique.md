**Prosecution (strongest case against):**

1. *Parsimony overshoot.* Iteration-3 adds: 3 primary species named, 3 secondary species mentioned, dedicated adjacent-mechanisms section, ethology grounding paragraph, Rogers' UPR as reference, signaling-theory mimicry framing, temporal arc for narcissism, species × attachment-variable profile schema, two-level naming convention, ecological framing for adjacent mechanisms, Cluster 4 elevation language. That's 11 distinct additions on top of iteration-2. The user's motivation was "iterative addition, not replacement" — this is closer to "iterative EXPANSION into a different-sized theory." The spec is growing faster than the evidence justifies.
2. *Species-collapse risk on D5.* Three primary species sounds taxonomic, but under scrutiny they may collapse. Self-Focus = attention on own priorities. Mission-Focus = attention on larger purpose. But "larger purpose" IS an own priority (chosen, embodied). Caregiver-Focus = attention on other's wellbeing non-extractively. But the caregiver has chosen this role — their attention IS on their own chosen role. If broadening "own priorities" to include roles, values, missions covers all cases, there's one axis with priority-content variations, not three distinct species.
3. *Adoption churn on D4.* User just internalized "Self-Focus" vocabulary in iteration-2. Iteration-3 introduces genus-level "Non-Extractive Attention" and promotes Self-Focus to species. Even with two-level naming mitigation, the user has to remember "the axis is Non-Extractive Attention at the theory level; my LinkedIn audit uses Self-Focus." Cognitive load on the user's practical application.

**Defense (strongest case for):**

1. *D1 wins decisively.* The counterexamples (mothers, beloved teachers, Gandhi, Mother Teresa, crisis workers) cannot be absorbed under Self-Focus-as-axis-name without significantly broadening the definition. The broadening would make "Self-Focus" mean "genuinely-held attention direction" — at which point the label is misleading. Naming the genus accurately (Non-Extractive Attention) is theoretically honest. Species preserve the useful distinctions (Self-Focus for value-establishing contexts; Caregiver-Focus for nurturance; Mission-Focus for cause-driven). Iteration-2's Self-Focus framing cannot handle these cases without theoretical compromise.

Signaling theory explains narcissism's exaggerated-then-decaying attachment arc, which iteration-2 couldn't. Ethology grounds the mechanism in observer-selection evolution. These aren't decorative; they predict phenomena iteration-2's framing couldn't.
3. *D8 extensibility.* The genus/species taxonomy accommodates future species without restructure. If iteration-4 surfaces (say) a ritual-focus or aesthetic-focus species, they slot in. Adjacent mechanisms have a named boundary they can cross if empirical work forces their inclusion. Iteration-2's single-axis framing would require re-architecture for each such extension.

**Collision:**

- Prosecution's *parsimony overshoot* is the strongest. The 11 additions are a lot. But several consolidate: temporal arc + signaling theory + mimicry are ONE story; ethology + ecology are ONE grounding lens; two-level naming is one decision. When consolidated, the real additions are: genus/species taxonomy + narcissism reconciliation with temporal arc + pro-social refinement + adjacent-mechanisms section + theoretical grounding paragraph + Cluster 4 elevation. That's 6 substantive additions. Still more than iteration-2's 4 edits. But iteration-2's scope was narrower (just the Self-Focus reframe). This iteration's scope (generalization testing across domains) is wider. The additions scale with scope.
- Prosecution's *species-collapse risk* is real for Self-Focus vs Mission-Focus but less so for Caregiver-Focus. The Caregiver-Focus / Self-Focus distinction passes the orthogonality test — a person can be Self-Focused without being Caregiver-Focused and vice versa, with distinct observable correlates. Mission-Focus may collapse into "Self-Focus where priorities include a larger purpose" — but this distinction matters in practice for APT Profiling (visionary leaders report differently than LinkedIn outreach targets). PARTIAL concession: Mission-Focus is the weakest species in the primary set; could be demoted to secondary.

Defense wins on D1 decisively. Partial ground to prosecution on D6 and D5 (Mission-Focus demotion recommendation).

**Defense:**

1. *D4 wins decisively.* User's iteration-2 vocabulary is preserved. 5-question audit, 2×2 typology, Self-Focus as axis label all unchanged. LinkedIn application continuity is maximum.

- Prosecution's D5 failure is decisive. A label that means "attention on own-priorities-including-chosen-roles-missions-values-transcendent-ideals" is no longer specifically Self-Focus — it's "any genuinely-held attention direction." The label becomes mis-named for what it now means.

- The compromise: ALPHA's two-level naming (theoretical Non-Extractive Attention + user-facing Self-Focus) serves the same D4 goal (user's practical application uses Self-Focus) while preserving D5 (theoretical label is honest about what it names).

**Position:**
- D1 Generalization Adequacy: PASS (if broadening holds)

- D6 Parsimony: PASS
- D7 Cluster 4 Honesty: BOUNDARY
- D8 Extensibility: BOUNDARY
- D9 Theoretical Grounding: BOUNDARY (harder to import external grounding)

**Verdict: KILL** on D5 (critical). The label-definition mismatch is structurally incoherent. The D4 benefit (user vocabulary continuity) is better served by ALPHA's two-level naming, which keeps Self-Focus at the user-facing level while using an honest label at the theoretical level.
