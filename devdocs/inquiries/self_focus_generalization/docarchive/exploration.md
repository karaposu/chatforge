## Cycle 2 — Probe Claim N (Narcissist-followers)

### What was probed

How does narcissistic self-absorption produce devoted followers — and how does it relate to the APT Self-Focus mechanism?

### Findings

**Narcissist's observable pattern:**

- Visible self-absorption (talking about self, not asking about others)
- Grandiose projection (I am special / superior / destined)
- Selective engagement (attention granted and withdrawn unpredictably)
- Violation of ordinary social reciprocity (doesn't follow "give as you receive")
- Entitlement displays (expects special treatment)
- Exploitative behavior (uses others for ends)
- Charm in initial encounters (love-bombing, intensity)
- Intermittent reinforcement (warm one day, cold the next)

**Internal state (from narcissism research — Kernberg, Kohut, Miller):**

Narcissists are NOT genuinely self-focused in the APT Self-Focus sense. Their attention is *extremely* other-focused in one specific way: they need external validation of their grandiosity. Every interaction is an approval-harvesting exchange at a higher stakes level (they need not just approval but worship). Their internal experience is a desperate maintenance of the grandiose self-image against threats of collapse.

**The critical distinction:**

- APT Self-Focus: attention on own priorities / agenda — non-extractive
- Narcissistic self-absorption: attention on own greatness being seen — extractive (from approval-harvesting POV)

**Yet the external display is similar:**

- Both appear to "not need" the other's ordinary approval
- Both selectively engage
- Both show disregard for conventional reciprocity
- Both have unavailability/scarcity quality

**So from the observer's POV:**

The observer sees the DISPLAY without access to the INTERNAL CONTENT. The observer reads: "this person's attention is elsewhere; they don't seem to need my approval." This reads like Self-Focus. The multiplicative gating mechanism fires. Attachment is generated.

**But the attachment narcissists generate is EXAGGERATED beyond what pure Self-Focus would predict.** Why? Additional mechanisms:

1. **Intermittent reinforcement** — narcissists' warm/cold cycles create variable-ratio reward schedules, which are well-documented to produce stronger attachment than predictable reward (behavioral psychology, slot-machine research).
2. **Grandiosity signaling** — high status/competence cue (dark-triad literature — narcissism correlates with perceived confidence).
3. **Social-norm violation signal** — someone who violates ordinary reciprocity signals "I am not bound by what binds others" → suggests high status / independent power.
4. **Initial love-bombing** — high-intensity early engagement creates investment asymmetry in the follower (the follower gave something that the narcissist was artificially returning, then withdrawal reveals the follower's investment).
5. **Trauma bonding** — cycles of abuse and reward create neurochemical attachment (dopamine-adrenaline-cortisol loops).

**Resolution of branch's three options for Claim N:**

- **Option (a)** (theory was wrong about "opposite" claim) — PARTIALLY TRUE. The pro-social reframe in iteration-2 conflated descriptive and prescriptive. At the descriptive mechanism level, Self-Focus and narcissistic self-absorption SHARE the Displayed-layer signal that observers read. At the prescriptive level (how the user should cultivate this), the distinction matters — healthy Self-Focus sustainably generates attachment that doesn't drain followers; pathological self-absorption produces the same attachment but exploits it.
- **Option (b)** (narcissists have Displayed Self-Focus without the internal content) — LARGELY TRUE. The visibility layer does the mechanism work for the observer's attachment generation. The internal truth is invisible to most observers early on.
- **Option (c)** (missing variable — additional mechanisms layered on) — ALSO TRUE. Narcissism is not JUST Displayed Self-Focus; it adds intermittent reinforcement, grandiosity signaling, social-norm violation, and other mechanisms. The exaggerated attachment requires them.

**Best resolution: (b) + (c) together.** Narcissists have Displayed Self-Focus WITH additional attachment mechanisms (intermittent reinforcement, grandiosity projection, etc.). The APT theory captures the Displayed Self-Focus contribution; the other mechanisms are *adjacent* and not yet in APT.

### Signals generated by probe

- **New signal:** Intermittent reinforcement is a distinct attachment-generating mechanism, adjacent to Self-Focus but not identical. Should be flagged for APT consideration.
- **New signal:** Grandiosity / high-status signaling is another adjacent mechanism (partially captured by Charm in APT, but Charm ≠ grandiosity exactly).
- **Theoretical signal:** The pro-social reframe in iteration-2 was implicitly prescriptive. Need to make this explicit — at the descriptive mechanism level, the theory is indifferent to whether Self-Focus is healthy or pathological.

### Confidence map update

- Narcissist-follower domain: CONFIRMED — Self-Focus theory explains the attachment mechanism via Displayed Self-Focus, but doesn't fully account for the exaggerated intensity. Additional adjacent mechanisms needed.

## Cycle 3 — Probe Claim D (Dating paradox)

### What was probed

The "caring dismisses attraction" pattern — same individual getting attraction when self-focused and dismissal when attentive/giving.

### Findings

**Pattern in dating literature:**

- Pickup artist corpus (problematic but observation-rich): "push-pull," "disqualification," "demonstrating higher value"
- Evolutionary psychology: commitment signaling research — over-committing before attraction is established signals low market value
- Attachment theory: anxious-avoidant cycles — anxious pursuer + avoidant pursued is a well-documented dynamic
- Relationship research: "Fisher, Aron, etc." on passionate love — unpredictability, challenge, and perceived inequality of investment are correlated with passionate attachment

**The mechanism chain:**

Man A (self-focused, doesn't chase):
- Attention on own life / agenda → Displayed Self-Focus high
- Woman registers: "he has his own thing going; he's not trying to extract my approval"
- Multiplicative gating: `attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(high Self-Focus)` → attachment amplified
- Woman's attention shifts toward him → she invests → investment creates further attachment (cognitive dissonance resolution: "I wouldn't invest if he wasn't worth it")

Same man B (attentive, giving, pursuing):
- Attention visibly on her approval → Displayed Self-Focus low (Supplication)
- Woman registers: "he wants something from me; his behavior is optimized for my response"
- Multiplicative gating: `attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(low Self-Focus)` → attachment collapses
- Additionally: his offered value (caring, giving) is devalued because of the modulator collapse
- Woman loses interest → cycle reinforces

**Theory fit: STRONG.** The Self-Focus mechanism directly predicts the asymmetry. The 2×2 diagnostic (Try-Hard vs Respected Expert quadrants) maps cleanly.

**Adjacent mechanisms also at play:**

- Commitment-market-value signaling: over-investing before attraction indicates low alternatives
- Intermittent reinforcement: unpredictable attention creates stronger attachment than predictable
- Scarcity: hard-to-access options are perceived as higher-value
- Investment asymmetry: getting her to invest → her psychological consistency locks her into caring

The Self-Focus mechanism is central but not exclusive. It's the load-bearing one — without Self-Focus, the others don't work either (you can't be scarce while also constantly reaching out).

### Important refinement from this probe

**The attention-direction axis is bidirectional in its effects:**

- Your Self-Focus → modulates *her* attachment signals to you
- Her Self-Focus → modulates *your* attachment signals to her

If both parties have high Self-Focus, the exchange has balanced modulators — attachment forms if f(charm,hope,fear) exists on both sides, but not with the asymmetric devotion the user's examples describe.

Asymmetric devotion requires:
- One party with high Self-Focus (modulator = high)
- Other party with low Self-Focus (supplicating, attention-harvesting)
- And f(charm,hope,fear) from the high-SF party toward the low-SF party

The "follower" dynamic requires asymmetric Self-Focus across the pair. The APT theory already predicts this via directional measurement (asymmetric/relational — Cluster 2 from iteration-1 innovation).

### Signals generated

- **Theory-refinement signal:** The asymmetric-devotion case (one worshipper, one worshipped) is structurally different from mutual-attachment (two parties with balanced modulators producing attraction). Current theory handles this via directional asymmetric measurement, but the finding could be more explicit about the asymmetric-devotion pattern specifically.

### Confidence map update

- Dating paradox domain: CONFIRMED — Self-Focus theory cleanly predicts the asymmetry; adjacent mechanisms (commitment signaling, intermittent reinforcement, scarcity) are co-active but the Self-Focus mechanism is load-bearing.

## Cycle 4 — Probe counterexamples (highest-priority tension signal)

### What was probed

Beloved teachers (Socrates, Mr. Rogers), caring mothers, Gandhi/Mother Teresa, crisis workers, devoted nurses — cases that generate intense devotion while appearing other-focused / giving.

### Findings

**Case: Socrates**

Attention: on truth / inquiry / philosophy. His conversations with students were structured around the philosophical pursuit, not around their approval.

From the student's POV: Socrates' attention is NOT on getting their approval or on extracting their admiration. It's on the question at hand. The student feels the attention is *with* them (in the shared inquiry) but *not on them* (not evaluating their reception).

This is **attention elsewhere from the interaction's ego-transaction** — but not self-focused in the narrow sense. The attention is on the mutual object (truth) that both are pursuing.

**Case: Mr. Rogers**

Attention: on the child's emotional development / the work of being genuinely present with children. Not on parents' approval, not on TV ratings, not on his own image. From the child's POV: "this adult is here for me" in a non-extractive way.

**Case: Caring mother**

Attention: on the child's wellbeing. Very high other-focus toward the child. But it is NOT extractive from the child's approval. Mothers don't audit children's reactions for mother-validation. Mother's attention is nurturant, not harvesting.

From the child's POV: "Mom's attention is on me AND it's non-extractive."

**Case: Gandhi / Mother Teresa / self-sacrificing activists**

Attention: on mission (national independence / non-violence / service to the poor / god / truth). Individual followers interact with them and sense: "this person's attention is on something bigger than my approval."

**Case: Crisis workers / nurses**

Attention: on the patient's medical needs / the work to be done. Not on patient approval. Patients feel cared for (attention IS on them in the caregiving sense) but not harvested (attention is not extracting their emotional response).

### Common structure across counterexamples

**The key insight:** In every counterexample, the attention is **non-extractive** — not oriented toward harvesting approval/validation/attachment from the current counterparty. But it's not specifically "self-focused." The attention can be on:

- One's own priorities / agenda (Self-Focus, narrow sense)
- A larger mission or cause (Mission-Focus)
- The work or craft itself (Task-Focus — performance-psychology frame)
- A third party's wellbeing (Caregiver-Focus — mother, nurse, devoted teacher)
- A shared object of inquiry (Dialogue-Focus — Socratic)
- Truth / god / some abstract ideal (Transcendent-Focus — saints)

All produce the same observer experience: *"this person's attention in our interaction is NOT on extracting my approval."*

### Theoretical implication

This converges on the Cluster E signal from iteration-2 innovation:

> "The axis may be fundamentally about HOW attention is held (extractive vs non-extractive / genuine vs evaluative / outcome-dependent vs outcome-independent) — not about WHERE it's directed (self vs other)."

The counterexamples are hitting the reopening conditions for Cluster E. Two of the three stated conditions:

- *"If user auditing reveals cases where attention-direction metrics don't reliably distinguish productive from anxious self-focus"* — the counterexamples show productive non-self-focused attention ALSO generating attachment, which attention-direction-as-axis mislabels.
- *"If the 'genuine vs evaluative' distinction surfaces in user auditing as the decisive factor"* — it's surfacing right here.

**This is a significant finding.** The generalization exploration is surfacing that the iteration-2 axis label ("Self-Focus" as attention-direction) may be a special case of a deeper mechanism (non-extractive attention / non-approval-harvesting).

### Signals generated

- **Major theoretical signal (HIGH importance):** The axis may need to be reframed from "attention direction (self vs other)" to "attention orientation (extractive vs non-extractive)." Self-Focus, Mission-Focus, Caregiver-Focus, Dialogue-Focus, Transcendent-Focus would all be species of the same genus: non-extractive attention. This is the iteration-3 refinement candidate.
- **Distinction signal:** Caregiver-Focus (mother, nurse) is distinct from Self-Focus but produces the same attachment mechanism in the counterparty. The APT theory as stated doesn't cover this case well.

### Confidence map update

- Counterexample cluster (beloved teachers, mothers, crisis workers, saints): CONFIRMED — produces the same attachment mechanism via a DIFFERENT attention-orientation (non-extractive, but not self-focused). Forces theoretical refinement.

**Job markets:**

- Candidates with other offers / appearing selective get better offers
- Attention is (or appears to be) on their other options, not on this one

**Animal training:**

- Cross-species validation — mechanism is not human-culture-specific
- Suggests the mechanism operates at a level more primitive than culturally-constructed status

**Customer service paradox:**

- Hard-to-reach companies often generate more loyalty than customer-service-heavy companies
- Apple's minimal support vs standard enterprise "please rate us 10/10!"
- Non-extractive corporate behavior generates attachment

**Academic citations:**

- Researchers with independent research programs get cited more than those who "beg" for citations
- Same mechanism — non-extractive scholarly behavior signals legitimacy

**Art markets:**

- Galleries requiring "request to view" — artwork gains perceived value
- The art/artist/gallery's attention isn't on selling you; it's on the art itself

### Theoretical implication

The mechanism generalizes broadly — to:
- Human 1-on-1 interaction (APT's original scope)

- Cross-species attachment (animal training)
- Institutional / market-level attachment (brand loyalty, negotiation, academic reputation)

This is a STRONG signal that the underlying mechanism is fundamental, not domain-specific. Suggests Self-Focus / non-extractive attention is a primitive psychological pattern (possibly evolutionary-rooted: reliable-independent actors are more valuable to social groups than politically-optimized ones — ethology finding).

### Signals generated

- **Generality signal (HIGH):** Mechanism operates across species and scales (individual, group, institutional, market). Supports theoretical weight.
- **Evolutionary signal:** The pattern may be evolutionarily primitive — social mammals preferring reliable-responsive-to-reality individuals over politically-optimized ones. This is the ethology insight from iteration-1 innovation (6.C) amplified.

### Confidence map update

## Cycle 6 — Probe extreme devotion cases

### What was probed

Cults, parasocial celebrity relationships, dictators, charismatic religious movements — cases of exaggerated / pathological attachment.

### Findings

**Cult dynamics (Lifton's "thought reform," Singer's cult research):**

Cult leaders combine:
1. Displayed Self-Focus (unavailability, selective engagement — often staged)
2. Grandiose mission claims (transcendent purpose)
3. Intermittent reinforcement (approval withheld, then granted, then withheld)
4. Social proof (existing devotees prove the leader's value)
5. Progressive commitment mechanisms (small yes → big yes)
6. Isolation from alternative attachment figures
7. Language and thought control

Self-Focus mechanism is present but layered with 6 other mechanisms. The exaggerated attachment requires the layering.

**Parasocial celebrity:**

Fan never meets celebrity. Celebrity's attention is necessarily "elsewhere" — on their art/life/craft. Displayed Self-Focus at maximum. No internal state is observable (celebrity could be deeply insecure; fan can't know).

This is the *cleanest* test of Displayed Self-Focus as a sufficient mechanism — the internal state is definitionally unavailable. Attachment still forms intensely.

Confirms: Displayed Self-Focus can generate attachment even when internal Self-Focus is absent or unknown.

**Dictators / demagogues:**

Historical cases — Hitler, Mao, Stalin, modern populists — combine:
1. Displayed Self-Focus (above ordinary politics, "only they can fix it")
2. Mission-Focus display (nation, revolution, destiny)
3. Charisma (Charm in APT)
4. Social-norm violations (signal of non-ordinary status)
5. Intermittent reinforcement / arbitrary power
6. Scapegoating and in-group/out-group dynamics
7. Spectacle / ceremonial performance

Again, Self-Focus is present but layered.

### Theoretical implication

Extreme devotion = Self-Focus (or non-extractive attention display) + multiple additional mechanisms. The theory captures one load-bearing component; full devotion dynamics require additive mechanisms.

For APT purposes: the theory is ACCURATE for its stated scope (one-on-one attachment mechanics). It doesn't need to be EXPANDED to cover extreme devotion — extreme devotion is just layered mechanisms on top of the same core.

### Signals generated

- **Scope-definition signal:** APT Self-Focus is one load-bearing component of attachment mechanics; extreme devotion requires additional mechanisms (intermittent reinforcement, social proof, grandiose mission, progressive commitment, isolation). These are adjacent theories, not necessarily part of APT.
- **Clean-test signal:** Parasocial celebrity relationships are the cleanest test — Displayed Self-Focus is sufficient to generate attachment even with internal state invisible.
