## Mechanism 3 — Inversion

### 3.A Generic — Invert "the refactor is necessary"

What if Self-Focus is still the axis and the counterexamples fit under it via a broader definition of "own priorities"?

- A caring mother's priority is her child's wellbeing. Her attention is on that priority. She IS self-focused in the sense that her chosen values/role drive her attention.
- A devoted teacher's priority is the student's development / the pursuit of truth. Self-focused on own chosen pursuits.
- A saint's priority is their relationship with the transcendent. Self-focused on own chosen orientation.
- Gandhi's priority is Indian independence / non-violence. Self-focused on own chosen mission.

Under this reinterpretation, ALL the counterexamples are Self-Focus cases where the "self" is expressed through deeply-held values/roles/missions rather than through narrow personal goals.

**Implication:** The axis could remain Self-Focus, with a broader definition: "Self-Focus is attention on one's own priorities, values, roles, and chosen pursuits — including pursuits that center on others or on transcendent ideals." This preserves iteration-2 vocabulary while absorbing counterexamples.

**Counter-argument:** This broadens "Self-Focus" to the point where it's indistinguishable from "genuinely held attention direction" — at which point Non-Extractive Attention is the more accurate label. But the preservation of vocabulary matters for adoption.

### 3.B Focused — Invert "Self-Focus is the user's primary for LinkedIn"

What if the user should actually lead with **Mission-Focus** or **Task-Focus** for professional outreach?

- Mission-Focus framing: "I'm reaching out because of this mission I'm working on" — attention is on the cause, not on self-display
- Task-Focus framing: "I'm working on this problem" — attention is on the work, not on self-display
- Self-Focus framing: "I'm attending to my own priorities" — could read as "I'm selfish"

For LinkedIn outreach specifically, Mission-Focus or Task-Focus may land better than Self-Focus because:
- Professional contexts reward commitment to work/cause
- Mission/Task framing naturally signals non-extractive attention (you're not there to extract approval; you're there because of the work)

**Implication:** For the user's LinkedIn case, Task-Focus might be the better named primary species than Self-Focus. Worth reconsidering the species-promotion question in P1.

### 3.C Contrarian (system-level) — Invert "APT is modulator-level; attention is secondary"

**Keep inverting (root-cause level):**

### 5.B Focused — What's missing in the narcissism reconciliation specifically?

The reconciliation says: narcissism fits Self-Focus at moment-to-moment + adjacent mechanisms produce exaggerated devotion.

What's missing: **the temporal arc of pathological attachment.** Narcissist-follower relationships have characteristic stages:
1. Love-bombing (high intensity) → attachment generated fast
2. Stabilization (apparent normalcy) → attachment reinforced
3. Withdrawal/devaluation (narcissist stops performing) → attachment intensifies via intermittent reinforcement
4. Cycles of abuse and reconciliation → trauma bonding deepens
5. Eventual escape or burnout → follower eventually leaves (sometimes after years)

APT's current framing is a static modulator model. It doesn't predict the TIME-EVOLUTION of pathological attachment.

**Implication:** A temporal-arc note in iteration-3 explaining that the modulator mechanism produces instantaneous attachment, but extreme devotion in pathological cases has TIME structure that is outside APT's scope. Handles the "why do followers eventually leave" question.

## Mechanism 6 — Domain Transfer

### 6.A Generic — Ecology / mutualism

Ecosystem biology distinguishes **parasitic**, **predatory**, **competitive**, and **mutualistic** inter-species relationships. Parallel to attention dynamics:

- Parasitic interaction: one party extracts without giving (narcissism — but with a veneer of giving)
- Predatory interaction: one party takes from the other at their cost
- Competitive interaction: both parties compete for same resource (attention/approval)
- Mutualistic interaction: both parties benefit from non-extractive engagement

Non-extractive attention is the individual-level precondition for mutualism. When both parties in an exchange are non-extractive, mutualistic dynamics emerge.

**Implication:** Grounds the theory in ecology. Scoping statement for adjacent mechanisms (P4) can use ecological vocabulary: "Adjacent mechanisms like intermittent reinforcement, trauma bonding, and aspirational distance can parasitize the reliability signal of non-extractive attention display. They represent signal-exploitation strategies evolutionarily adjacent to genuine non-extractive attention."

### 6.B Focused — Carl Rogers' Unconditional Positive Regard

Rogers proposed UPR as the core therapeutic mechanism: the therapist's non-judgmental, non-conditional, genuine acceptance of the client. This is a specific form of non-extractive attention — Caregiver-Focus with explicit methodology.

Rogers identified three conditions:
1. **Congruence** (therapist is genuine, not performing)
2. **Unconditional positive regard** (acceptance of the client's experience without extraction)
3. **Empathic understanding** (attention truly on the client's world)

All three describe Caregiver-Focus operationally. Rogers spent a career specifying how this attitude is cultivated, what threatens it (counter-transference, burnout, over-identification), and what sustains it.

**Implication:** Caregiver-Focus species has a ready-made operationalization via Rogers' framework. The iteration-3 spec can reference Rogers' three conditions as the detection criteria for Caregiver-Focus. Rich pedagogy inherited.

### 6.C Contrarian — Signaling Theory / Evolutionary Arms Races

Signaling theory (Zahavi, Maynard Smith) — costly signals evolve precisely because they can't be faked cheaply. But mimicry also evolves — cheap signals that mimic costly ones arise.

Applied:
- Genuine non-extractive attention is a costly signal (you pay: foregone optimization for the exchange)
- Narcissism is a signal-mimicry strategy — displays of non-extractive attention without the actual cost (via intermittent reinforcement + grandiosity + etc.)
- Evolutionarily, mimicry is detected over time (the full cost isn't actually paid), and observers develop counter-signals

**Implication 1:** This explains WHY narcissist-follower relationships eventually burn out — mimicry is detected after sufficient exposure. Temporal arc from 5.B gains evolutionary grounding.

**Implication 2:** The theory should explicitly address the mimicry vs genuine distinction. Iteration-3 spec could add: "Displayed Non-Extractive Attention is the visibility signal observers read to generate attachment. Over short timescales, genuine and mimicked displays produce similar attachment. Over longer timescales, the full cost (or lack) of non-extractive attention becomes detectable, and mimicked displays produce attachment-decay rather than sustained attachment."

**Implication 3:** This answers the pro-social refinement more completely. The mechanism IS pro-social when genuine (because it's evolutionarily favored as reliability signal). It's anti-social when mimicked (which observers detect over time). The two cases produce similar IMMEDIATE attachment but diverge in SUSTAINABILITY.
