# Branch: self_focus_generalization

## Question

Does the Self-Focus / Self-Positioning framework (established in `apt_self_focus_reframe/finding.md`, built on `apt_missing_dimension/finding.md`) generalize to explain interpersonal phenomena beyond the original LinkedIn / value-establishing-conversation case — specifically:

1. **The narcissist-follower / worship dynamic:** Narcissistic individuals often accumulate followers / devotees / worshippers who are intensely attached, despite (or because of?) the narcissist's self-absorbed behavior.
2. **The dating paradox (the "caring dismisses attraction" pattern):** A man who is visibly self-focused / doesn't-chase can generate strong attraction and compliance from women, even without material advantages (wealth, looks), while the same man becoming attentive / giving / pursuing often triggers dismissal.
3. **Other observable patterns** across workplaces, social hierarchies, communities, fandoms, mentor-disciple dynamics, etc.

— and if so, which domains does it cover cleanly, what boundary conditions emerge (where does it fail or need qualification?), and does the wider application surface any theoretical refinements that would trigger an iteration-3 update to the Self-Focus finding?

**Nuance:** One aspect worth making explicit — the user's observation implies a *potentially uncomfortable* theoretical claim: if Self-Focus explains narcissist-follower dynamics, the theory is partly explaining why *pathological* self-absorption produces attachment (not just calibrated self-focus). The disciplines should not shy away from this.

## Seeds (material for the loop)

**The theory to test (from `apt_self_focus_reframe/finding.md`):**

- **Self-Positioning** is a multiplicative modulator on Domain 1 (Attachment) in APT.
- **Self-Focus** axis: attention on own priorities/agenda (positive pole) vs Supplication (attention on securing the other's response — negative pole).
- **Displayed Self-Focus**: the visibility principle — internal state must be observable in behavior.
- **Mechanism:** `Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(self-focus)`. When g collapses (attention flips to securing-their-response), attachment is near-zero even with high f. Failure signature = devaluation.
- **Pro-social reframe (from iteration 2):** Self-Focus here is narcissism's *opposite* — narcissism is politically-optimized, intensely attuned to others' reception; Self-Focus is not. **THIS CLAIM IS NOW BEING TESTED AGAINST THE USER'S COUNTER-OBSERVATION.**
- **Appropriate-low-Self-Focus caveat:** genuine vulnerability, transparent asymmetry, intimate warmth, ceremonial contexts.

**The user's specific claims to evaluate:**

1. **Claim N (Narcissist-followers):** Narcissists accumulate attached followers despite self-absorbed behavior. Competitors (smart, helpful, attractive but non-self-focused individuals) don't. → Self-Focus explains this asymmetry.
2. **Claim D (Dating paradox):** The same man gets attraction/compliance when self-focused, dismissal when attentive/giving. → Self-Focus explains this reversal.

**A tension to resolve up front:**

Iteration 2's pro-social reframe explicitly positioned Self-Focus as "narcissism's opposite." Claim N implies narcissists ARE doing something Self-Focus-like that produces attachment. Two possible resolutions:

- **(a) The theory was wrong about the opposite claim** — narcissistic self-absorption and calibrated Self-Focus share the same attachment mechanism; the spec's pro-social framing was a reassurance move, not a structural claim.
- **(b) The theory is right; what narcissists have is "Displayed Self-Focus" (the visibility signal) without the internal content** — their attention is actually on others' reception (politically-optimized) but the external behavior (selective engagement, disregard, unavailability) *looks like* Self-Focus and produces the same modulator effect.
- **(c) There's a missing variable** — narcissism has something else (e.g., perceived scarcity, authority projection, violating ordinary social rules) that combines with apparent Self-Focus to produce exaggerated attachment.

The loop should resolve this explicitly.
