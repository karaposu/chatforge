# Branch: self_focus_dynamics

## Question

What are the **operational dynamics of Self-Focus** at the moment-to-moment interaction level — specifically:

1. **Character (micro → macro aggregation):** Self-Focus leaves an observable mark on others; accumulated across interactions, this shapes how people remember each other — what we normally call **character**. How does Self-Focus aggregate into character-perception, and does this belong in APT's theoretical frame?

2. **Approach dynamics (5-minute windows):** In short first-contact interactions (cold approach, pitch, introduction), there isn't enough time for Charm/Hope/Fear signals to fully develop — yet outcomes diverge sharply between individuals. Two men can approach the same woman with similar looks/credentials; one gets reciprocation, the other gets dismissal. The theory predicts this turns on *how Self-Focus is conveyed* in the moment (word choice, posture, dress, tone of premise: "please be with me, you're above me" vs "I like how you look, I want you"). Is the approach case a special case of the existing theory, or does it require operationalizing a distinct sub-mechanism?

3. **Neediness paradox:** A needy person is also self-attention-directed (attending to their own lack). But neediness is *unattractive* — it fails the modulator the same way Supplication does. Why? What distinguishes **productive Self-Focus** (attention on own agenda/craft/desire — amplifies attachment) from **needy self-attention** (attention on own lack/wanting-from-them — collapses attachment)? Both are self-directed.

4. **Attention-giving paradox:** Within established relationships (girlfriend, boss, close friend, child), *giving attention* to the specific other is both expected and healthy. Yet the theory says non-Self-Focus collapses the modulator. How do these coexist? Is attention-giving simply diminishing Self-Focus, and does that only matter when Self-Focus was established in the first place? Or is there a more nuanced dynamic — Caregiver-Focus species (established in iteration-3), or context-dependent calibration, or something else?

5. **Calibration check:** How does one know what is the *right amount* of Self-Focus for a given context? By what observable or felt signal can the user check whether their current calibration is too much or too little?

## Goal

A coherent framework that answers all five sub-questions and gives the user:

- **Theoretical clarity** on the distinction between productive Self-Focus and neediness (both are self-directed; only one produces attachment)
- **Operational criteria** for the approach-style short-interaction case: what specific signals convey productive Self-Focus in a 5-minute window
- **An integration** of character-perception with the existing Self-Focus theory (does "character" belong as a layer of APT, or is it aggregated observation outside APT's scope?)
- **Calibration heuristics** for knowing when Self-Focus is too much, too little, or appropriate — across different relationship types (approach, established, hierarchical, caregiving)
- **A resolution** of the attention-giving paradox that reconciles legitimate non-Self-Focus (Caregiver-Focus for established relationships) with the modulator's predicted collapse when Self-Focus is low

Strong enough to either add a dedicated "Operational Dynamics" section to the existing finding (`self_focus_generalization/finding.md`) or identify that an iteration-3.1 refinement is warranted.

## Scope Check

Question covers goal: **YES** — but with one nuance worth flagging, not widening.

The question's 5 sub-parts all concern operational dynamics of a concept (Self-Focus / Non-Extractive Attention) whose architectural identity is already settled by iteration-1 through iteration-3. The goal asks for framework + operational criteria + calibration + resolution. These map cleanly onto the 5 sub-questions.

**Nuance:** Sub-question 4 (attention-giving as diminishing Self-Focus, "maybe what attention IS is diminishing self focus") hints at attention-economy substrate framing — which is iteration-3's Cluster 4 reopening territory. The disciplines should handle this at the operational-dynamics level of this inquiry WITHOUT triggering Cluster 4 reopening unless the operational dynamics force it. Disciplined scoping.

**Likely theoretical contribution:** This inquiry probably produces a distinction the spec doesn't yet make cleanly:

- **Self-Focus (productive):** attention on own agenda / craft / desires / values — independent of counterparty's response
- **Needy self-attention:** attention on own lack / wanting / deficit — DEPENDENT on counterparty's response to relieve

Both are self-directed in surface description. The difference is whether the attention is **self-sufficient** or **other-dependent-for-resolution**. This maps onto the Extractive/Non-Extractive axis that iteration-3 named at the genus level — neediness is Extractive self-attention (extracting one's own lack-resolution from the other), which is why it behaves as Supplication and collapses the modulator.

If this framing survives the loop, the iteration-3 finding gains an important clarification: Self-Focus species is NOT "attention-on-self"; it is "attention-on-own-priorities-independent-of-this-exchange's-resolution."

Exploration and sensemaking should test this and refine.

## Seeds (material for the loop)

**The theory being probed (from `self_focus_generalization/finding.md` — iteration-3):**

- **Genus:** Non-Extractive Attention (attention NOT on harvesting approval/validation/response from the current counterparty)
- **Primary species:** Self-Focus (own priorities — value-establishing contexts) + Caregiver-Focus (non-extractive attention on counterparty's wellbeing — mothers, therapists, teachers)
- **Secondary species:** Mission-Focus, Task-Focus, Dialogue-Focus, Transcendent-Focus
- **Negative pole:** Supplication / Extractive Attention (approval-harvesting)
- **Mechanism:** multiplicative gating `Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(non-extractive attention)`
- **Failure signature:** devaluation
- **User-facing application:** Self-Focus axis for LinkedIn diagnostic; 5-question audit; 2×2 typology (Try-Hard / Confident Selector / Disengaged / Respected Expert)

**Candidate framings for the disciplines to test:**

*For Q1 (Character):*
- (a) Character = aggregated species-tendency across interactions → belongs in APT Profiling spec extension
- (b) Character = broader than Self-Focus (includes values, moods, cognitive style) → APT contributes but doesn't cover
- (c) "Character as Self-Focus shape" is a user observation to record but scope-out theoretically

*For Q2 (Approach):*
- (a) 5-minute approach is a special case of Self-Focus species operationalized at maximum resolution (the 6 text-feature metrics + body-language extensions)
- (b) Approach requires its own sub-mechanism ("first-contact modulator") distinct from established-interaction mechanisms
- (c) Approach reveals that Self-Focus operates even in the ABSENCE of Charm/Hope/Fear signals — confirming the modulator's multiplicative gating precedence

*For Q3 (Neediness):*
- (a) Neediness = self-attention that's still EXTRACTIVE (wanting from the other). Surface label ("self") doesn't match mechanism (extractive). Theory needs to sharpen: Self-Focus = attention on own priorities INDEPENDENT of counterparty's response. Neediness is Supplication with self-flavored language.
- (b) Neediness = low Self-Focus with deceptive surface (attention on self's lack appears self-focused but is other-dependent)
- (c) Neediness is a separate failure mode requiring its own mechanism

*For Q4 (Attention-giving):*
- (a) Attention-giving in established relationships = Caregiver-Focus species (already in iteration-3). Non-extractive attention TO the other without approval-harvesting.
- (b) Attention-giving alternates with Self-Focus — oscillation is healthy; sustained imbalance isn't
- (c) Attention-giving as diminishing Self-Focus IS attention (the user's hypothesis) — hints at attention-economy substrate reframe (Cluster 4 territory)

*For Q5 (Calibration):*
- (a) Check: does counterparty's attention increase or decrease when you withhold / engage? (Meta-signal)
- (b) Check: is your attention genuinely on own priorities or on extracting response? (Internal audit)
- (c) Check: does the exchange produce sustainable value-creation (both parties benefit) or drain (one extracts from the other)?
- (d) Context-dependent calibration: value-establishing contexts favor high Self-Focus; established trusting relationships favor Caregiver-Focus; crisis/mentoring favors different mixes
