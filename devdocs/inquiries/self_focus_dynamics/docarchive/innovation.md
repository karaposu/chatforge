### 6.B Focused — Stoic dichotomy of control

Epictetus's dichotomy of control: distinguish what's up to you (thoughts, judgments, responses, actions) from what's not (external events, others' responses, outcomes).

**Direct parallel to iteration-3.1:**
- What's up to you = your own priorities, agenda, attention-direction
- What's not = counterparty's response
- Self-Focus = attention on what's up to you
- Outcome-independence = not attaching your state to what's not up to you
- Productive self-focus = accepting what's not up to you as unresolved by external response

**Practice protocol (importable):**
- Daily Stoic journaling: "What today was up to me? What wasn't? Did I confuse them?"
- Evening review (Marcus Aurelius model): after an interaction, distinguish what the counterparty did (not up to me) from what I did (up to me), and check whether my internal state is tied to theirs
- Pre-exchange preparation: "What's my agenda here? What would constitute success independent of their response?"

**Implication:** Stoic practice protocols are a mature, imported pedagogy for cultivating outcome-independence. Worth reference in iteration-3.1's prescriptive-guidance layer with a pointer to Stoic practice (without deep integration or theological claims).

#### Section 2: Definition Tightening (P1)

Independence-clause wording: **"independent of counterparty's response to resolve them"**

Applied:
- **Self-Focus species** = attention on own priorities independent of counterparty's response to resolve them
- **Caregiver-Focus species** = non-extractive attention on counterparty's wellbeing independent of harvesting their approval (the "independence" here is from approval-extraction, not from the counterparty as object)
- **Mission-Focus species** = attention on a larger cause independent of current-counterparty's response to the cause
- **Task-Focus, Dialogue-Focus, Transcendent-Focus** = inherit same pattern
- **Supplication / Extractive Attention** (inverse) = attention whose resolution IS dependent on counterparty's response

Genus-level definition unchanged (non-extractivity is already the defining property).

Contrast examples:
- Productive Self-Focus: "I'm working on X; here's what I think about it" — whether or not you engage, I continue
- Needy self-attention: "I'm feeling lonely / worried / wanting; tell me I'm okay" — your response determines my state
- Caregiver-Focus: "I care about how you're feeling" — without needing your approval for caring
- Approval-harvesting dressed as care: "I'm attending to you so you'll like me" — attention on you is instrumental to their approval

#### Section 3: Mechanism Temporal Detail + Multimodal Metrics (P2)

Temporal-precedence paragraph:

"The multiplicative gating formula `Attachment ≈ f(charm, hope, fear) × g(non-extractive attention)` operates at every moment. Its temporal profile has one important property: g operates at **instantaneous display-bandwidth** (posture, voice tone, opening premise, eye contact, response calibration are all readable within seconds), while f develops over longer time-horizons (Charm/Hope/Fear accumulate as content builds). In short windows (first 5-30 seconds of first-contact), the g signal dominates the attachment-probability because f hasn't fully developed. This is why 5-minute approaches are decided by Self-Focus display rather than by Charm depth."

Multimodal metrics catalog extension:

| Metric | What it measures | High-g signal | Low-g signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Posture | Body-language grounding | Relaxed, upright, own-centered | Fidgety, closed, leaning-toward |
| Voice cadence / pitch | Speech-pattern self-regulation | Calm, unrushed, lower-pitched | Rushed, pitched-up, apologetic |
| Eye contact pattern | Gaze-regulation | Steady, unhurried, direct | Darting, avoiding, over-eager |
| Body alignment | Spatial-orientation | Oriented-at from confident stance | Looming-toward, pursuing-with-body |
| Response-timing tempo | Reactivity-tempo | Unhurried response; comfortable pauses | Immediate-reactive; no silence tolerance |
| Exit readiness | Physical-comfort-with-ending | Comfortable with short, natural close | Clingy, extending, reluctant |

Placement: as a parallel "Multimodal Display Metrics" subsection to the existing text-feature metrics catalog. Both together operationalize Displayed Non-Extractive Attention across text and embodied contexts.

#### Section 6: Calibration Audit (P4) — Tiered

**The gateway 1-question audit:**

> *"Am I okay if they don't respond favorably?"*
>
> If a genuine YES: your non-extractive attention is likely well-calibrated; continue.
>
> If NO (any form of collapse, anxiety, or spiral without their response): use the full 7-point audit to diagnose the specific miscalibration.

**The full 7-point audit** (independent-composite):

1. **Context check:** What context am I in? (value-establishing / established / hierarchical / caring / performance / cause-driven / dialogue / contemplative)
2. **Species check:** Which non-extractive-attention species is active for me?
3. **Species-context fit:** Is this species appropriate for this context? (see Section 4 table)
4. **Outcome-independence:** Am I okay if they don't respond favorably? (the decisive test)
5. **Internal state:** Was I grounded or anxious during the exchange?
6. **Counterparty-response curve:** Was their attention rising or falling through the exchange?
7. **Exchange quality:** Mutual benefit or extraction / drain?

Interpretation: independent-composite — each check catches a distinct miscalibration dimension. If all pass: high confidence. If any fails: that check identifies the specific miscalibration.

Relationship to iteration-2's 5-question audit: SUPPLEMENT (not replace). The 5-question audit remains the quick LinkedIn diagnostic. The 7-point audit is for finer calibration across contexts.

Forward-looking value: as user applies the audit across 20-50-100 interactions, personal subtype patterns emerge (specific Try-Hard sub-types; specific miscalibration triggers; context-specific strengths). The audit becomes a pattern-surfacing tool, not just a diagnostic.
