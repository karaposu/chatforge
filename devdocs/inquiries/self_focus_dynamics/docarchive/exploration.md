## Cycle 2 — Probe Q3 (Neediness paradox)

### The probe

What IS neediness mechanically, relative to iteration-3's Non-Extractive Attention framework?

**Iteration-3 framework:**
- Genus: Non-Extractive Attention (attention NOT on harvesting approval/validation/response from current counterparty)
- Self-Focus species: attention on own priorities
- Supplication (negative pole): attention on securing counterparty's response

**The neediness scenario:** Person feels lonely / inadequate / wanting connection. They direct attention to their own loneliness / inadequacy / need. In surface description, this is self-directed attention. They then interact with another person while carrying that attention state. The interaction fails — the other person dismisses, withdraws, or loses interest.

**Mechanical analysis:**

A needy person's internal focus is on their own lack. But the lack is defined by what the counterparty CAN PROVIDE. The mental structure is: "I lack X; you can give me X; if you give me X, my state is resolved; if you don't, my state worsens." The attention is self-directed in CONTENT (I'm thinking about my own lack) but other-dependent in STRUCTURE (my state's resolution is contingent on your response).

This is **Extractive Attention with self-flavored content.** The genus label (Non-Extractive) exposes this correctly — the person's attention is NOT non-extractive (they need extraction for resolution). They fit the Supplication pole of the axis, not the Self-Focus species.

The confusion arises because the label "Self-Focus" reads literally as "attention on self." But in iteration-3's theoretical structure, Self-Focus species = a specific type of *non-extractive* attention whose content happens to be on one's own priorities. The non-extractive condition is PRIMARY; the self-content is a descriptor of one subtype within non-extractive.

**Refinement surfaced:**

The iteration-3 Self-Focus species should be defined as:

> **Self-Focus = attention on one's own priorities INDEPENDENT of the counterparty's response to resolve them.**

Key clause: *independent of counterparty's response to resolve*. This makes the non-extractive-ness explicit in the species definition, preventing the neediness misread.

**Contrast:**
- **Self-Focus (productive):** attention on own agenda, desires, craft. Self-sufficient. You continue whether they respond or not.
- **Needy self-attention:** attention on own lack, wanting. Other-dependent for resolution. You collapse/spiral if they don't respond.
- **Supplication (classic):** attention explicitly on securing the other's response. Other-directed content, other-dependent structure.

Neediness is structurally equivalent to Supplication despite different surface content. Both produce `g(low)` on the multiplicative gating.

### New signals from Cycle 2

- **Definition-tightening candidate:** Self-Focus species definition should explicitly include "independent of counterparty's response to resolve." This sharpens iteration-3 without restructuring.
- **Sub-territory reveal:** "Outcome-independence" emerges as a core property of productive non-extractive attention. This should feature in the calibration framework (Q5).

### Confidence update

- Q3 territory: **CONFIRMED** — neediness is Extractive self-attention, fits Supplication pole, does not fit Self-Focus species. Refinement needed to iteration-3 definition.

## Cycle 3 — Probe Q2 (Approach dynamics — 5-minute windows)

### The probe

User's claim: in 5-minute approaches, Charm/Hope/Fear don't have time to fully develop, so Self-Focus is the load-bearing first-contact modulator.

### What each variable can deliver in 5 min

- **Charm** in 5 min: a witty line, a sharp observation, a visible marker of competence (clothes quality, vocabulary, demeanor). SOME delivery, not full — depth needs longer exposure.
- **Hope** in 5 min: the other can sense prospective benefit only broadly — "this person seems interesting, we might have something to offer each other." Specific material hope (resources, connections, career help) isn't visible in 5 min.
- **Fear** in 5 min: essentially zero. Fear requires asymmetric power dynamics that take time to establish. A stranger has no fear-based hold on another stranger in 5 min.

### What Self-Focus (or its absence) can deliver in 5 min

At MAXIMUM bandwidth:

- **Posture** — confident / relaxed / grounded (high) vs fidgety / closed / anxious (low)
- **Voice tone and cadence** — calm / assertive / unhurried (high) vs rushed / pitched-up / apologetic (low)
- **Opening premise** — selecting / offering / asserting (high) vs requesting-permission / over-qualifying (low)
- **Eye contact pattern** — steady / unhurried / direct (high) vs darting / avoiding / over-eager (low)
- **Response calibration** — continuing own thread / not tracking her every reaction for approval (high) vs adapting everything to her feedback / monitoring (low)
- **Exit-willingness** — comfortable with a quick "good talking, enjoy your evening" (high) vs clingy / extending / needy (low)
- **Body alignment** — oriented at her from confident stance (high) vs looming-toward / pursuing-with-body (low)
- **Conversation topology** — bringing content he cares about (high) vs testing which topics she likes to stay on (low)

These micro-signals are ALL displayed within 5 minutes. They don't need time to build.

### The specific test: two men, same looks, different outcomes

Man A:
- "I noticed you're reading X. I was just thinking about Y related to it. Curious what you think." (selecting / offering premise)
- Unhurried voice; steady eye contact; relaxed body; okay if conversation is short
- If she shows disinterest: "Cool, enjoy your reading" and moves on without reactivity

Man B:
- "Hi, I hope I'm not bothering you, I just noticed you from across the room and thought you looked really interesting..." (requesting-permission; self-justifying premise)
- Rushed voice; eye contact darts; body leaning forward; extending conversation beyond natural endpoints
- If she shows disinterest: tries harder, over-explains, becomes apologetic

Man A generates attachment; Man B doesn't. Same looks, same context. The difference is 100% in Self-Focus display.

### Mechanism confirmation

The 2×2 diagnostic's vertical axis (Self-Focus level) operates at 5-min resolution. The horizontal axis (Investment-Asymmetry) also operates — Man A's low initial investment + high Self-Focus = **Confident Selector** (upper-left quadrant). Man B's high initial investment + low Self-Focus = **Try-Hard / Supplicating** (lower-right quadrant). Exactly the mechanism iteration-2 named.

**The temporal prediction holds:** Self-Focus is the first-contact modulator because its display operates at instantaneous bandwidth, while Charm/Hope/Fear require time to build.

### First-contact modulator implication

This is worth naming explicitly in the theory: the modulator has **temporal precedence** over the attachment variables in the multiplicative gating. Attachment doesn't form gradually as Charm/Hope/Fear accumulate — it's GATED from the start by whether Self-Focus display (or whichever Non-Extractive Attention species is contextually relevant) produces g(high) or g(low). If g collapses in the first 30 seconds, even strong Charm/Hope/Fear won't recover the exchange.

### New signals from Cycle 3

- **Temporal-precedence signal:** multiplicative gating operates from first display; attachment variables can't overcome an initial `g(low)` regardless of later content. Worth explicit statement in the spec.
- **Approach-specific diagnostic:** body-language and tonal signals are part of the catalog for approach contexts. iteration-3 catalog is text-focused; approach context needs multimodal extension.

### Confidence update

- Q2 territory: **CONFIRMED** — Self-Focus dominates 5-min windows because of temporal precedence of its display. Multimodal extension of operational metrics is warranted for approach contexts.

## Cycle 4 — Probe Q4 (Attention-giving paradox)

### The probe

How does legitimate attention-giving (to girlfriend, boss, child, close friend) coexist with Self-Focus? If giving attention to another person is non-Self-Focus, does it collapse the modulator?

### Scenario analysis

**Scenario A:** Man with girlfriend he's been with 2 years. Listens to her about her work stress. Attention is on her, her words, her state. Non-Self-Focused in attention direction. Relationship doesn't collapse — it strengthens.

**Scenario B:** Employee listening attentively to CEO's strategic vision. Attention on boss's words. Non-Self-Focused. Employee is seen as engaged and loyal.

**Scenario C:** Parent fully present with crying child. Attention completely on child. Non-Self-Focused. Attachment deep.

**Scenario D:** Person in new dating context giving all attention to the other to "show interest." Non-Self-Focused. Dismissed.

Why do A, B, C work but D fail?

### Resolution candidates

**(a) Caregiver-Focus species (iteration-3):** Scenarios A-C are Caregiver-Focus in operation. Non-extractive attention on the counterparty IS non-extractive — the genus property is satisfied. g(high) because the attention isn't harvesting approval; it's genuine care/engagement. D is different — the attention is subtly extractive (hoping for her interest / approval / reciprocation), which puts it in Supplication despite surface similarity.

**(b) Context-establishing vs established distinction:** In value-establishing contexts (new relationships, approaches, first contact), Self-Focus dominates because mutual value hasn't been established yet. In ESTABLISHED contexts (with proven mutual value), Caregiver-Focus sustains. Different contexts, different species-fit.

**(c) Oscillation within interaction:** Healthy interaction oscillates between species. In A: man with girlfriend alternates between listening to her (Caregiver-Focus) and sharing his own experience (Self-Focus). Neither dominates exclusively. Sustained imbalance in either direction fails — pure Self-Focus reads as cold; pure Caregiver-Focus over time can read as sycophantic or dependent.

**(d) Attention-as-diminishing-self-focus (user's hypothesis):** Points toward Cluster 4 substrate territory. Under attention-economy reframing, all attention is scarce resource allocation, and "giving attention" is directing the scarce resource away from self. Consistent with but not forced by the current observations.

### Probe result

**Combined (a) + (b) + (c) resolves the paradox without invoking (d):**

The apparent paradox dissolves once we apply the iteration-3 genus correctly. Legitimate attention-giving in established relationships is Caregiver-Focus species — it's NOT non-extractive attention direction (genus property satisfied). What it's NOT is Self-Focus species specifically — but it doesn't need to be. The genus holds; the species shifts.

Context-dependent species-fit:
- **Value-establishing (new relationship, approach, sales first-contact)** → Self-Focus is primary; Caregiver-Focus might come later
- **Established trusting relationships (partner, close friend)** → Caregiver-Focus + Self-Focus oscillate; both active
- **Hierarchical context (employee-to-boss)** → attentive listening to boss's agenda is Task-Focus (attention on the work, not on boss's approval) or Caregiver-Focus depending on relationship
- **Performance/craft contexts** → Task-Focus dominates
- **Caring/service** → Caregiver-Focus dominates

The user's observation "if you don't give attention to some people it also hurts relations" is correct and captured by the iteration-2 caveat (appropriate-low-Self-Focus in established relationships) + iteration-3's Caregiver-Focus species. In established relationships, sustained pure Self-Focus reads as cold/indifferent; Caregiver-Focus oscillation is actively needed.

### The critical clarification

Scenario D fails NOT because attention-giving is bad, but because the attention in D is not actually Caregiver-Focus — it's Supplication dressed as attention. The person is attending to her not non-extractively but extractively (hoping she'll respond favorably). The surface appearance is attention-giving; the mechanism is approval-harvesting. Same genus/pole distinction as the neediness case.

### New signals from Cycle 4

- **Species-context-fit framework:** different contexts favor different species as primary. This is a natural extension to iteration-3 that sharpens the prescriptive layer.
- **Oscillation dynamics:** healthy interactions oscillate between species within exchanges. Sustained imbalance in either direction is pathological.
- **The attention-giving trap:** attention-giving in value-establishing contexts is often Supplication dressed as Caregiver-Focus. The genus test distinguishes them.

### Confidence update

- Q4 territory: **CONFIRMED** — paradox dissolves under iteration-3's Caregiver-Focus species + context-fit + oscillation dynamics. No Cluster 4 substrate reframing required at this level.

Each person HAS a recognizable attention-orientation signature, and this signature contributes significantly to the "character" I attribute to them. - Self-Focus shape (stable non-extractive-attention-species-tendency) = the aspect of character that's visible in every interaction

Character in full includes values, moral disposition, cognitive style, and specific choices — but the Self-Positioning bearing is particularly visible because every interaction exhibits it, while other character dimensions emerge only under specific conditions. APT Profiling outputs capture the Self-Positioning bearing component of character-perception; the rest is outside APT's scope.

## Cycle 6 — Probe Q5 (Calibration check)

### The probe

What signals tell you whether your Self-Focus is calibrated right for the current context?

### Candidate checks generated

**(a) Counterparty-response signal (within-interaction)**
- Are they leaning in? Does their attention increase when you withhold/pause?
- Too-high Self-Focus (cold/aloof): their attention fades; they disengage
- Too-low Self-Focus (needy): their attention pulls back; they distance
- Right calibration: their attention engages, maintains, occasionally increases

**(b) Internal state signal**
- Am I grounded, calm, unhurried? (healthy Self-Focus)
- Am I anxious, rushed, monitoring their every reaction? (low Self-Focus — their-approval-harvesting)
- Am I indifferent, disengaged, detached? (too-high Self-Focus — or actually disengaged, not-present)

**(c) Exchange-quality signal**
- Is the exchange producing value for both? Mutual?
- Do I feel energized or drained afterward? (drain signals wrong calibration somewhere)
- Do they seem sustained by the exchange or diminished?

**(d) Context-fit signal**
- Does this context call for Self-Focus species, Caregiver-Focus species, Task-Focus, Mission-Focus?
- Am I operating in the right species for this relationship/moment?
- Value-establishing context → Self-Focus primary
- Established trusting → Caregiver-Focus oscillating with Self-Focus
- Hierarchical / professional → Task-Focus or Self-Focus depending on role
- Mentoring/parenting → Caregiver-Focus primary

**(e) Longitudinal sustainability signal**
- Over time, does this relationship feel sustainable?
- Cycles of abuse/reconciliation → mimicry is being detected (narcissism pattern)
- Gradual drift apart → Caregiver-Focus absent when needed (if established relationship)
- Sustained trust and growth → calibration is probably right

**(f) Outcome-independence signal** (the decisive Self-Focus test)
- Can I continue whether they respond favorably or not?
- If their non-response collapses me → I wasn't actually Self-Focused; I was needy in self-directed language
- If their non-response is absorbed without system-level dysregulation → my Self-Focus was genuine

### Multi-check framework

No single check is sufficient; different checks catch different miscalibrations:

- (f) catches productive-vs-needy self-attention distinction
- (a) catches within-interaction imbalance
- (b) catches internal-state misalignment
- (c) catches relationship-level health
- (d) catches context-species mismatch
- (e) catches long-term pattern

### Proposed user-facing calibration audit

After any interaction the user wants to audit:

1. **Context check:** What context was I in? (value-establishing / established / hierarchical / caring / performance)
2. **Species check:** Which species was active for me? (Self-Focus / Caregiver-Focus / Task-Focus / Mission-Focus / Supplication)
3. **Species-context fit:** Right species for this context?
4. **Outcome-independence check:** Am I okay whether they respond favorably or not?
5. **Internal state check:** Was I grounded or anxious during?
6. **Counterparty-response check:** What was their attention curve doing through the exchange?
7. **Exchange-quality check:** Did both parties seem to benefit? Am I drained?

If ALL signals point to well-calibrated, confidence is high. If any single signal is off, that signal identifies the specific miscalibration dimension.

### New signals from Cycle 6

- **Multi-check framework** is the answer to Q5 — no single check, but a composite audit addressing different miscalibration types
- **Outcome-independence is the decisive test for productive-vs-needy** — completes the Q3 loop
- **Context-species-fit is the decisive test for attention-giving appropriateness** — completes the Q4 loop

### Confidence update

- Q5 territory: **CONFIRMED** — multi-check framework provides calibration, with specific checks catching specific miscalibration dimensions.
