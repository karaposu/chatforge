## Phase 1 — Cognitive Anchor Extraction

### Constraints

- **C1** — The reframe must explain the devaluation failure mode that the iteration-1 finding established: without the modulator, high charm/hope/fear gets discounted post-hoc ("this guy doesn't deserve this"). Any new framing that can't explain this is wrong.
- **C2** — The reframe must preserve the internal-external dual character that sensemaking in iteration 1 established (Ambiguity 4 resolution). A purely external (behavioral) framing or purely internal (state-only) framing fails.
- **C3** — The reframe cannot be symptom-named (inherited from iteration 1's C2). Symptoms of the attention-direction (e.g., "not-helpful-ness", "terseness") are not valid names for the concept.
- **C4** — The reframe must explain why *performed* versions of the behavior fail (try-hard reads). In iteration 1 this was the CV example's decisive evidence. The Self-Focus framing must hold up against the same evidence.
- **C5** — The reframe must map coherently to the 5-signal catalog (Withholding, Premise-Posture, Self-Justification-Density, Exit-Willingness, Rhythm-Comfort) or must justify why those signals change.
- **C6** — The reframe must respect Path α architectural placement (named modulator between Domain 1 and Domain 2) — or must provide structural reason to change.
- **C7** — The user's proposal is two-part: *kendine odaklanmak* (behavioral/directional — where attention goes) **+** *gözükmesi* (visibility — it must be observable). Both parts are required. Evaluating Self-Focus without the visibility component would miss half the proposal.
- **C8** — The reframe must resolve the vocabulary concerns from iteration 1 (Self-Elevation's "prescriptive arrogance" risk, R1 in iteration 1's sensemaking) better than or equal to Self-Elevation — otherwise the gain is illusory.

### Key Insights

- **KI1 (causal vs state)** — Causal descriptions are genuinely stronger than state descriptions in theory-construction. They (a) explain why the phenomenon emerges, (b) generate testable predictions about when it appears/disappears, (c) suggest interventions. "Self-Elevation" says *what* the posture is. "Self-Focus" says *what produces* the observable behaviors. The latter is theoretically richer.
- **KI2 (causal chain)** — Self-Focus explains the iteration-1 finding's external correlates cleanly: *attention-elsewhere → no optimization for their approval → reactions show freely → responses are calibrated from own priorities → charm/hope/fear signals don't carry neediness → no devaluation*. Self-Elevation describes the endpoint without naming the chain.
- **KI3 (visibility is load-bearing)** — "Gözükmesi" is not decorative. Internal self-focus without visible manifestation doesn't work — the other party has to perceive the attention pattern. This is the same insight as iteration 1's costly-signaling theory (innovation 2.C), but stated in positive-directional form (where attention *is*) rather than negative-directional (what's *not* shown as need).
- **KI4 (what the user rejected)** — The user explicitly rejected "Self-Regard" (sounds like ego/self-esteem) and "Investment Control" (sounds strategic/calculated). These are legitimate semantic concerns. The reframe is partially motivated by avoiding semantic mis-landings that would push users toward performed arrogance or calculated withholding — neither of which produce the genuine state.
- **KI5 (the anxious-distracted test)** — Someone focused on their own *anxiety about being judged* is self-focused in a directional sense but produces entirely different behavior than someone focused on their own *agenda*. This is the sharpest test of the Self-Focus framing. If it can't distinguish these cases, it needs qualification.
- **KI6 (visibility resolves the anxious case)** — Anxious self-focus is visible as distress signals (hedging, apologizing, over-explaining, rushed rhythm). Confident self-focus is visible as the opposite. The "gözükmesi" requirement distinguishes the productive case without needing a separate qualifier — anxious-self-focus *doesn't look like* self-focus in its displayed form.
- **KI7 (alignment with iteration-1 supplementary theories)** — Iteration 1's critique accepted visible-need-concealment as MEDIUM-confidence supplementary mechanism. The user's Self-Focus proposal is structurally adjacent (where visible-need-concealment says "no visible need," Self-Focus says "visible focus on own priorities"). This is not a new idea arriving from nowhere; it's a refinement of a direction the prior SIC loop already sensed.
- **KI8 (narcissism-connotation risk in English)** — "Self-Focus" in English has slight pejorative loading ("he's so self-focused" ≈ selfish). In Turkish *kendine odaklanmak* is more neutral. For an English APT spec, the label may need attention. But the *concept* is independent of the label.

### Structural Points

- **SP1 (two framings of one phenomenon)** — Self-Focus (causal/directional) and Self-Elevation (state/hierarchical) are not two independent axes. They describe the same phenomenon at different levels of abstraction. Self-Focus is upstream (the mental state that produces the pattern); Self-Elevation is the stable-bearing observable result.
- **SP2 (orthogonality test against Self-Focus)** — Can you have high Self-Focus with low Self-Elevation? Yes — narcissistic self-absorption or anxious self-preoccupation. Can you have high Self-Elevation with low Self-Focus? Dubious — if you're "big" but attending to their approval, you're not actually positioned big in any effective way. This suggests Self-Focus is the broader concept and Self-Elevation is a *subtype* (a particular kind of self-focus), not a separate axis.
- **SP3 (visibility as the interface)** — "Gözükmesi" is the bridge between the private attention direction and the public observable behavior. This maps directly onto the internal/external dual character iteration 1 committed to. Internal = attention direction; External = displayed self-focus (the behavioral surface).
- **SP4 (the causal chain is testable)** — From Self-Focus, five consequences emerge: scarce investment, unmanaged reactions, absence of over-giving, amplified attachment variables, preserved value. These are specific and observable. The Self-Elevation framing produced similar external correlates but by assertion, not derivation.

### Foundational Principles

- **FP1** — Causal explanations > state descriptions. If two framings cover the same data, the causal one is preferred because it has more predictive power.
- **FP2** — Genuineness matters (preserved from iteration 1's FP3). The transcript's evidence rules out any framing that would work when performed — only genuine internal states produce the effect.
- **FP3** — Visibility is a second-order requirement. Internal state without observable manifestation doesn't produce the modulator effect. The "gözükmesi" constraint is not optional.
- **FP4** — Vocabulary has pragmatic consequences. Labels that mis-land push users toward performance of the wrong thing. Self-Regard → ego; Self-Elevation → arrogance; Self-Focus → (ambiguous in English).

### Meaning-Nodes

- **MN1 — Self-Focus** (where attention lives — the causal root)
- **MN2 — Displayed Self-Focus** (the visibility requirement — external-correlate layer)
- **MN3 — Causal chain** (attention → consequences → devaluation prevention)
- **MN4 — Anxious vs confident self-focus** (the disambiguation problem, resolved by visibility)
- **MN5 — Self-Elevation** (from iteration 1 — the state-level description this reframe supersedes)

## Phase 3 — Ambiguity Collapse

### Ambiguity 1 — Is Self-Focus a mechanism or a state?

**Resolution:** Self-Focus is a **directional mental state with causal structure** — not purely one or the other. Attention is a state (you have attention pointed somewhere at any moment), but its direction is causally consequential (it determines which information is processed, which emotions are reacted to, which responses are generated). This is more powerful than pure state ("I am big") because it carries derivable consequences.

**Counter-interpretation test:** Could Self-Focus be framed as pure mechanism (a process) without state content? E.g., "attention allocation process." This strips the directional content that makes the concept specific. Unhelpful.

**What's now fixed:** Self-Focus is a state-with-causal-structure — a directional mental state. Its causal power comes from attention's role in cognition and behavior selection.
**What's no longer allowed:** Treating Self-Focus as either pure state ("I'm self-focused") OR pure mechanism ("focus-allocation happens") — it's the directional state that has mechanism-like consequences.
**What depends on this:** Axis naming should reflect directional character. Quality check applied below.
**What changed in the model:** The insight that not all "state" descriptions are equivalent — directional states carry causal content that hierarchical states don't.

---

### Ambiguity 2 — Does Self-Focus supersede, complement, or proxy Self-Elevation?

**Resolution:** **Supersede, with the visibility requirement doing the disambiguation work.** Self-Focus and Self-Elevation describe the same phenomenon at two abstraction levels. Self-Focus is upstream (causal description); Self-Elevation is downstream (stable-state observable result). They're not two independent axes — collapse to one axis with causal-directional framing (Self-Focus).

**Counter-interpretation test (strongest):** Could they be two independent axes?
- Can you have high Self-Focus with low Self-Elevation? Narcissistic self-absorption, anxious self-preoccupation → these are "self-focused" directionally but produce different outcomes.
- Can you have high Self-Elevation with low Self-Focus? Dubious — if your posture is "big" but you're attending to their approval, the elevation is performative, not effective. The "elevated" posture without self-focused attention doesn't produce the modulator effect.

The asymmetry (Self-Focus is broader, Self-Elevation is a subtype) suggests they're not independent. The "narcissistic / anxious self-focus" cases don't mean we need two axes — they mean Self-Focus needs qualification (which the visibility requirement provides: productive self-focus looks different externally than narcissistic/anxious self-focus).

**Structural reason the counter fails:** If they were independent axes, the 2×2 cross-product would produce four meaningfully distinct cells. But the "high-elevation + low-self-focus" cell collapses — it's performative elevation, not a real phenomenon. And the "high-self-focus + low-elevation" cell is internally heterogeneous (covers both anxious and grounded self-focus — visibility discriminates). So the Self-Focus × Self-Elevation cross-product is degenerate; they're not independent.

**What's now fixed:** Collapse to one axis. Self-Focus is the causal-directional framing; Self-Elevation is the stable-state framing. They're not two things to track separately.
**What's no longer allowed:** Treating Self-Elevation as a distinct axis alongside Self-Focus (would double-count).
**What depends on this:** Only one axis label needed; choice between Self-Focus and Self-Elevation.
**What changed in the model:** Simplification — the perceived two-ness of the framings is illusory; they're one phenomenon at two description levels.

### Ambiguity 4 — Does the umbrella stay "Self-Positioning"?

**Resolution: YES — keep Self-Positioning as umbrella.**

The umbrella label names the structural role of the concept within APT — it's a modulator at the positioning level, parallel in naming convention to Domain 1 (Attachment) and Domain 2 (Presentation). Self-Positioning is a structural-role label. Self-Focus is a mechanism label. They live at different levels of the spec.

If the umbrella becomes Self-Focus:
- Spec readers see "Self-Focus" as a concept name without the role-signal
- The parallel with "Attachment" / "Presentation" (domain-level names) weakens
- Future modulators (Tempo, Affect, Coherence) would need their own umbrella names; "Self-Positioning" establishes the pattern that modulators get position/role labels

If the umbrella stays Self-Positioning and the axis becomes Self-Focus:
- Structural role stays legible
- The axis name carries the mechanism content
- Clean separation between what-it-is-structurally vs how-it-works

**Counter-interpretation test:** Could we drop Self-Positioning entirely and just call it Self-Focus? Loses the structural signal. Spec readers wouldn't know immediately that Self-Focus is a modulator at the positioning level.

**What's now fixed:** Two-level naming. Umbrella = Self-Positioning (structural role). Internal axis = Self-Focus (causal mechanism). External correlate layer = Displayed Self-Focus (visibility requirement).
**What's no longer allowed:** Flattening to single-level naming (either just Self-Positioning or just Self-Focus).
**What depends on this:** finding.md section structure — Self-Positioning section has a Self-Focus axis and a Displayed-Self-Focus correlate layer.
**What changed in the model:** The umbrella / axis / correlate three-level structure is made explicit (implicit in iteration 1).

## SV6 — Stabilized Model (Final)

### The Decision

**Adopt the Self-Focus reframe at the axis level via Path (B).** Keep Self-Positioning as the umbrella; replace Self-Elevation with Self-Focus as the internal axis label; add Displayed Self-Focus as an explicit external-correlate layer that captures the visibility requirement.

### The Conceptual Structure

```
Self-Positioning  ← umbrella (structural role: modulator at positioning level within APT)
├── Self-Focus  ← internal axis (causal mechanism: where attention genuinely lives)
│                  - attention on own priorities → produces modulator effect
│                  - attention on their response/approval → collapses modulator
└── Displayed Self-Focus  ← external correlate layer (visibility: the mechanism
                            must be observable in behavior to produce the effect)
                            - manifests as the 5-signal catalog
                            - distinguishes productive self-focus from anxious look-alikes
```

### The Causal Story (replaces the iteration-1 state-level story)

When your attention in an interaction is genuinely on your own priorities (your agenda, your reactions, your sense of what matters) — not on securing the other's response or approval — the following derive naturally:

- **Investment is calibrated low** — you don't over-give because the exchange isn't your priority
- **Reactions show freely** — you're not managing their feelings; your reactions are your information, not their experience
- **Over-giving is absent** — you don't offer more than the exchange requires, because the exchange isn't where your attention is
- **Charm, hope, fear signals amplify** — when present, they carry the credibility of a person attending to their own life rather than auditioning for this one

The failure signature (**devaluation**) emerges when attention flips: attention on their response → signals read as optimized-for-approval → the other perceives asymmetric-need → discount applied post-hoc ("this guy doesn't deserve this").

The visibility requirement is load-bearing: internal self-focus without observable manifestation doesn't produce the modulator effect. The other party has to perceive the attention pattern. Performed self-focus (trying to appear self-focused) produces distress signals, not modulator signals — the two are distinguishable on the behavioral surface.

### What This Resolves vs Iteration 1

- **Iteration 1 said:** Self-Elevation is the internal axis; external correlates emerge from it; dual character is load-bearing.
- **Iteration 2 says:** Self-Focus is the internal axis; external correlates *derive causally* from it; dual character is load-bearing; the internal state is now named at the causal-mechanism level, not the posture level.

The architecture is unchanged. The mechanism is unchanged. The signals are unchanged. The description — and specifically the name — of the internal driver shifts from *state* ("elevated posture") to *causal direction* ("attention on own priorities").
